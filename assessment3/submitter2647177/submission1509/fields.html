The game was a wonderful exercise on the applied aspects of key components of risk management. With the game exercise, concepts learnt in the sessions (theory) so far are deeply seated in my mind.

Risk management has two goals, a) to build resilience which aims at enhancing people’s ability to recover from negative shocks, and b) to build prosperity which incorporates people’s response to positive change.   The game includes both the goals. The damage caused by comets attack and community efforts in coping is a reflection of the same.

One of the obstacles in preparation is lack of resources or lack of information. Knowledge, protection and insurance helps in preparing in advance. And coping can be a community effort. With the available resources, the game made me strike a judicious mix of expenses on research, and protection. I had spent on research keeping in mind severity and chances of attack of comets along with reserving fund for insurance. The game required strategic agility and tested ability to strike a balance between preparedness and coping with limited resources as well. The preparedness for risk aided in countering damage to pods (but this I could understand in my second attempt).

In my later attempts, I could understand community development acted as a major source of protection and hence I could reserve funds on upgrading pods (some) and insurance. But repair for upgraded pods was a costly affair. Hence research and insurance were more suitable.  

Risk has uncertainity associated with it. At times even despite prior knowledge (comet attack), and protection, damage (to pods) can happen. At that time insurance and working jointly i.e. the community network helps in coping.

With reference to my area, prior knowledge and protection with instinct to act (behavioral) is needed as despite evident signs of risk, procrastination or indifference is the first approach.  In case of negative shocks, community network and efforts from state would be need of the hour.
