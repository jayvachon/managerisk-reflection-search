Risk game reflection:

What I learned from playing the game as well as listening to the video lectures are that Risk Management is a very useful to have but not the easiest thing to accomplish.  Balancing various elements to accomplish the goals is very challenging.  The role of risk management in the game is critical to be successful.  It requires a strategic thinking to advance from level to level.

I had a hard time increasing the protection level to match comet’s damaging level. I felt I was not able to use all three items freely. That is the only part of the game that was frustrating to me.  I believe I can do a better job advancing if the protection level works better.  I could balance better the tradeoffs on the game for each situation (comet potential hits).  So having a good knowledge is great to assess what actions to take for that particular event.

I played the game several times and able to learn and do things differently to see if the outcome can be better for me.  First I did not buy enough insurances, I learned without enough insurance it costs more to build as well as it cost more time to build it.  Then I over bought the insurance and wasted the resources which in turn slows down the building of community.

So I try to balance all of the elements, buy enough insurance, continue with building the community and have enough protection for the negative event.  In doing so, you can then advance to the next level. I believe this is the right approach of Risk Management. 

When you thought you have a good strategy, you ran out of time.  This happens several times during the games.  I realize that in the real life events, all of these parameters exist and one must be able to assess the situation properly to mitigate the negative events when happen. After playing the game I have a better understanding the critical role of the government when natural disasters occur.  In Japan, the tsunami thread is large and they are able to put in place the warning system as well as practice of simulated event, yet the other risk like the nuclear plant ruptured are not part of it.

Here in Arizona, we have minimal natural disasters such as tornadoes, earth quake, etc… but we do have issue with limited water resources. So if we have a prolong draught, we can have a negative impact with our lives.  It would be wise for our state government to have risk management to mitigate the situation.  We are also a growing state, many people migrated to our state.  This also will add pressure to the water resources.  Balancing development, insurance as well as protection is a difficult task to do but an important thing to have. I hope our state has a risk management in place.
