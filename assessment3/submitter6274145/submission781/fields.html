Question 1

In my opinion, risk management in the game (and in the real life) consists in getting a balance amid knowledge, protection, insurance and to cope with shocks.

Some of my main conclusions –which imply a trade-off amid the concepts exposed- in the game, than yor can apply to the world too, are:

A – Knowledge: knowledge is esential to try to reduce the shock’s damage. Severity and probability of the shock are key points that we should take into account. Anticipation and preactive behaviour are both attitudes that we should have. For instance, if we had a shock that won’t have a deep impact, probably good information could be enough to reach cheap solutions.Sometimes, knowledge and properly evaluation can require less resources and be as effective as other ways. On the other hand, maybe we shold focus in that shocks that have more probability to take place (For example: in a deser we should care mor about water scarcity than about floods). We should be prepared for shocks before they appear. In this point, knowledge about History, enviroment,... it´s esential.

B – Protection: Often, knowledge is not enough. For shocks with a deeper impact, we need preventive measures, which usually involves more resources. Not only economic resources, but also human and material ones. Obviously, there is a trade-off. If we use resources in these measures, they will not be availables for other purposes (as economic growth,...). So, it’s quite important identify risks/shocks, and to develop the right measures. Investing less than neccesary could lead to disaster. Investing more implies wasting resources.

C -   Insurance: If knowledge and protection that we can afford aren’t enought, then we should purchase some insurance, in order to cover part of the losses.

D – Coping with shocks: if shocks finally hits us, then we have to cope with consecuences, and take time to repair everithing. One thing I’ve learned is resource scarcity makes everithing more complicated. There’s the importance of having biggest cities, biggest societies and biggest countries. Then, it’s possible to share expenditures and to take more efficient measures. If it isn’t the case, cooperation is also a good choice.

E – Finally, the most important is a balance sheet amid knowledge-protection-insurance- to cope with shocks
