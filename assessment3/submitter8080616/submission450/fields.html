The goal of the game was to establish a higher and higher developed society from one stage to the other. Since development cost money it was necessary for one to distribute the available amount between the development investment and either rising the living space to save the house from floods or researching the incoming threats. The main focus was for me to develop the houses to meet the level goals. Because of this I started and build only a few settlements in the beginning to see how costly their upgrades would be. After this I started to research the meteoroids, focusing on the once with the higher damage and/or hit threat. Furthermore it was important for me to make sure my houses were at least insured to a rate of 50%. Even though I did this in every level, I only needed the insurance’s help in the first round, since I waited with rising the living area until the second stage. I have the feeling, that it was very important to have the financial security the insurance offered me and paid me back 50% of the damaged. This gave me the opportunity to invest more money in the buildings and in particular in research.  After having played the game I realize that rising the living area above ground is a measure to safe the settlement in last second but at the same time comes at a high cost. The cultural development is stalled for the moment and money is lost in the process. After a while it became more important for me to focus on research and leveling the buildings, though I did not follow this insight enough in my last round and sacrificed too much of my money and even more of my development on rising the ground, which lead to failing the round’s goal within the set time limit. The most (cost-) effective measure really was to do proper research on the relevant threats. This way I could avoid most damages to my houses just be being reasonably prepared. Concluding from these experiences and since I played the game only once I would definitely only rise the ground/protection in the first and second round. Research and quick response in the case of a hit seem to be the better choices in respect of developing the houses in the given time limit. The concept of “protection” was not really clear to me right from the beginning and I needed to figure out was the switch was doing, what eventually hampered my development. In addition I think the emergency mechanic of connecting the houses in case of a hit seems to be a bit clumsy even though the idea behind it is absolutely reasonable.
