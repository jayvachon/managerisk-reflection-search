1)     In the game of Risk Horizon, we get three kinds of risk management: knowledge, protection and insurance.

Knowledge is embodied by the research on each comets, taken separately. The research gives some results about the probability that the comet would damage Alora, their severity and the time before impact. It gives the player a precious tool to help him make his decision. Personally, I systematically used the research on the comets. If the chance of the comet hitting Alora was high, I would try to prepare in advance a better protection and so try to match the severity with the level of Alora. And if I didn’t do it before, I would subscribe the insurance at the highest level. If it was very low, then I would purely ignore it, but I would still subscribe the insurance, anyway.  

The protection is more something on the long term. While doing so, the surface of Alora is being lifted up little by little. I would regularly lift it up, while sustaining development at the same time. Of course I would lift it faster if some danger was imminent and if the severity was higher than the altitude of Alora. This part takes some money and time and so it clearly impedes development on the short run, but it is so important that at the end it prevents the development from being stopped and even taken aback.

And as for the insurance part, despite all the precautions we take, the protection we’re doing, sometimes there are just some inevitable disaster, and we have to protect ourselves against those at least financially. The experience on this game taught me that we should not be stingy about taking an insurance, and I always took the most expensive formula, that is to say the protection against 75% of the damage. And I have always paid less money for the insurance that what the insurance finally paid for me. So, it is better not to hesitate to take it. I would say that the money it takes to subscribe it impedes the development, but it speeds up the recovery speed after a disaster and less money is being invested at the end thanks to it.

 

2)     I played the game more than once. Since the beginning, I used research and insurance as I explained, without changing anything in the further trials of the game. What changed the most was my use of protection and the way I developed the planet. To be frank, I didn’t understand at all how protection did work so I used it at first only when an asteroid was about to damage the planet. But then, I finally understood and used it as I explained in the first part. As for the development, the first game, I built all level 1 pods, then I was planning to update lvl 2 only after I would have finished all level 1 pods. But in the late parties, I gradually update pods and let some vacant place for further levels. There are several explanations to this. First is that is there is some damage, less places are damaged and so the costs are reduces. Secondly, as the updates go up, the costs go up as well, and the time it takes to build it also goes up. In doing so, we can leverage the building phase in terms of available money and time.


3)     I think if I had to add something to the game, it would be about introducing people to the game, people that could act on their own but we still would have the same possibilities in game. Simply that sometimes, the people would not listen to us. And since it would be harder to accomplish our task, we should add as well the possibility to incentive people to do something the way we want.
