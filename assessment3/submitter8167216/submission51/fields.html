The game was a way to compare and select actions to prevent future damage and losses. Between the available possibilities, the player should evaluate which actions are immediately necessary, or if an action, e.g. lifting the island, can be seen as a future investment.

Playing the game more times gives also the right feeling to weigh better the actions. Sometime an expensive insurance that cover the 75% of damage is not necessary, and a cheaper insurance can be used saving money that can be used for other investments.

The main things that I learned in the game are: the time play a key role and always we must understand when an action has the major effect; moreover there is always a trade-off between the possible actions, and quite often putting the maximum effort, or resources, to one single action (e.g. insurance) may not be the optimum solution in a long period view, we always must look for a good coordination among the actions.

In the real life a good research and preparation can almost reduce to very low level the impact of a foreseeable event, and I would emphasize more this aspect a new game.
