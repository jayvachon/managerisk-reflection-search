It is amazing how much you can learn about risk management only by playing a simple game. When I saw that this week's assessment will consist in playing this game I thought it will not be big deal, but actually it was an efficient way of making some practice on what we studied so far.

I think that the role of risk management in this game managed to summarize the entire essential elements which have to be taken into account in a real life situation.  I had to make many tradeoffs, choosing between protection and development, the best equilibrium between protection, knowledge and insurance. Preparing for risk affected the way development advanced because I had to use many for preparation, money that I could use instead for improvements, slowing my progress in the game. Also the decisions had to be taken fast, because the time was going off quickly.

Second time when I played the game I used the ability of making research more often, in order to make a decision regarding if I should use my resources for development or protection. But all these were using my time, and focusing on so many facets in a short time was pretty demanding.

I learned from this game that risk management is not as easy as it seems when it is discussed just theoretically. Moreover, it is very important to understand how protection, insurance, research affect the development and to weight the decisions carefully, prioritizing the most important actions. I also learned that protection gives you a lot of confidence and sometime we have the tendency of just protecting ourselves and not using insurance or research to gain more information and knowledge. But this extra protection will just cause a slow development rate, making things progress slower. I also learned that first time it is not possible to make it perfect, and the way to perfection means trial and error. Making decisions and then observe their effect. If the decision brings good results that decision will be taken more often, otherwise it will be not taken anymore and other options will be explored.

If I would be supposed to develop a new version of the game I would make it a bit closer to the real life situations that people confront with in the area where I leave. It would make them realize much better how important risk management is and they will understand that the choices they make and the decisions they take regarding this are extremely important to avoid the cruel effects of shocks and to be able to purse opportunities for development.
