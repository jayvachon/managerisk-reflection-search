1) What was the role of risk management in the game? What choices did you make and what trade-offs did you confront in terms of knowledge, protection, and insurance? In what ways did preparation for risk affect development (immediately, or after some time)?

The role of risk management in the game was implementing the different tools that we have in risk management: knowledge, protection, insurance and coping strategies.

The choices I made during the game in terms of knowledge were studying the most dangerous comets (the ones that had a high level of severity) in order to reduce the damages in my island; in terms of protection I decided to use the feature of raising the ground, and in terms of insurance I decided to acquire one of the three plans of insurances that were in the game. I tried to balance those aspects in order to use the resources wisely.

The preparation for risk affected the development of my planet after some time in the very first levels of the game but it affected immediately during the levels 4, 5 and 6 (preventing the meter filled at an optimum rate).

 

2) If you played the game only once, what would you do differently if you were to play it again? Alternatively, if you played the game more than once, what did you do differently after the first time? What were the main things you learned from the game?

After several tries, I decided to build a few pods (instead of trying to build all of them) and updated the pods up to level 3. Then I built the rest of the pods and updated them as well up to level 3. When I had all the pods with level 3, I started to update them all, one by one up to the maximum level. Also I decided to obtain an insurance only when I saw that there was a clearly risk (i.e. when more than two comets were approaching with a high level of severity). And I used the protecting option only a few times per level, trying not to lose too much production in order to pass the level.

The main things I learned from the game are two: the first one is that even the more expensive insurance won’t protect you 100% from  a potential risk and the second one is that the capacity of researching is one of the most powerful items that we as human beings have for protecting ourselves and our environment.

 

3) The game captures, in a simplified way, some aspects of risk management. Imagine you are tasked with developing a new version of the game, which allows you to change or add one simple thing. What change would you propose to better represent the risk management choices that matter in the place you live?

Although I consider the game captures all of the aspects of risk management (the tools of knowledge, protection, coping strategies and insurance), I will add one more feature into it: maintenance. In the country I live nowadays, we have a lot of infrastructures for risk management, but sometimes they just don’t receive the proper maintenance, which produce inefficiencies. So I think during the game, one of the aspects shouldn’t be just updating the pods but also to maintaining them.
