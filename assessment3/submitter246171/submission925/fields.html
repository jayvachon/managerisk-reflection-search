It is obvious that the game was designed to train risk management skills and it had it almost all – probability and impact of the risk, research (gaining knowledge), insurance and protection and limited resources. I would confess that it was quite difficult for me to manage all the possibilities and to find the right balance. I actually did not succeed in finding it. I managed the levels from 1 to 3 but passing level 4 was impossible for me. The reason was that the severest impact were just at the end of the game were despite the knowledge, the protection and the insurance the impacts were so big that I actually did not have the time to rebuild what was lost. It tried to build different strategies by managing the level of the different components – knowledge, protection and insurance, it tried different building strategies but all was in vain. The huge impacts at the end of the 4 level destroyed all my efforts. I played the game several times even on different computers. What I noticed was that on some computers the level of protection was increasing with different speed. On one of the commuters I was not able to raise the land enough to light the firs bar on the right.

I few words should e said on the game itself. It covers the basic understanding of risk management and it gives some tools to train risk management. What I couldn’t find was the opportunities given by the emerging risks. I also could not understand the way the money in the account was increased. Was it a rent from the units build? Was it random? Because money and time have very restrictive influence on the risk management strategy. So restrictive that it was impossible for me to find the winning strategy.

If I had to design the game I would definitely try to make it a little bit easy to find the winning strategy for each level. As the game is designed at the moment the learning is quite difficult and there for the knowledge gained by the player is very limited. I would definitely create a feedback loop to help the player see the pros and cons of each strategy and to help him compare the different approaches. Other wise most of the players will be suck only to basic knowledge and skills in risk management.
