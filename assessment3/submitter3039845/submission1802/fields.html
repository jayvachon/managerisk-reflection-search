Question 1

Risk management provided the framework for making the trade-offs required to advance development in the face of risks.  This framework made it easier to think through the issues with regard to striking an appropriate balance between protection (stopping bad things from happening) and insurance (making sure that the impact was relatively small when bad things inevitably did happen).

One of the most challenging trade-offs related to the question of how much insurance would be adequate.  In some cases it was possible to make this call based on the threat posed by the comets that were visible on the screen but this did not deal with the longer term and the “deeper” uncertainty that this presents. In a similar manner, the question of how much protection would be sufficient to deal with the threat.  Too much insurance or protection imposes an opportunity cost on how much development could be undertaken.

 

The knowledge component was easier to deal with and could be systematised.  Comets were subject to a rapid assessment and prioritised based on the speed it was moving, the probability of impact and the probable severity of the impact.  The focus on gaining knowledge was directed at those comments that posed the most likely, immediate and severe threat.

 

Question 2

I played the game twice: the first game was characterised by seeing the impact of a focus on a single element of risk management and the impact of that.  The one element that I did not get to grips with the first time was the building of connections between pods. The approach the second time around was based on a better understanding of the environment and the rules of the game; and included the element of strengthening connections.  The strategy was consequently more multi-faceted and “sophisticated”. (Not sophisticated but relatively more sophisticated than the first time around) and I easily passed the second level where I had failed the first time.  The key learning is that experience counts and some reflection (monitoring and evaluation) enables the development of the risk management approach.

 

Question 3

Something simple that could be added to the game that would further add to the game would be an extra function that enable the nature of the shock to be dealt in alternatively ways.  The risk chain diagram suggests that the nature of the shock can be changed by the outcomes of risk management. Currently the comets arrive like clockwork regardless of anything that can be done in terms of insurance, protection or knowledge.  In reality, there are interventions that can pre-empt the shock or at least change its nature. For example, one could add a missile to the game that could destroy comets before they arrive or break them in half and reduce their impact. Obviously the missile would be very expensive and come at a cost of how far and fast development could be advanced.
