Appropriate risk management in the game allowed for further and faster development of the community. By managing the risk of comets causing damage to the pods, less had to be spent on repairs and thus more financing was available for upgrading the infrastructure. However, the balance between managing risk and developing the community was dependent on how the resources were used. The different resources available were knowledge (research), protection and insurance.

 I chose to invest heavily in protection as it had a long-lasting effect in contrast to knowledge which was limited to a specific comet only. Buying insurance helped reduce the cost of repairs in case of damage but was not sufficient to achieve the balance between development and risk management. Whenever time was available, I invested in research as it allowed for damage to be minimized – the community links created through the research aided to reduce the damage and thus less financing had to be dispensed for repairs. Investing in lasting protection enabled development to be achieved faster in the long run.

After playing the game more than once, I decided to build as many pods as possible before the threat of comets got more severe. Whilst trying to keep a small amount of money as reserve, I also increased the amount spent on protection. Additionally, I minimized the amount spent on repairs by taking a basic insurance package and making use of the community links to the best of my ability.

The main lesson from the game encompasses the strain faced when deciding whether to spend resources on development or on risk management. By spending up front on protection, resilience could be achieved. Nevertheless, the return from buying the expensive protection is not captured immediately but it is realised over time. To achieve the right balance, I had to devote funds to both development and protection whilst also trying to make the best use of the other resources available. Whilst a formal cost-benefit analysis was not possible due to time limits in the game, the approach I followed consisted of a similar logic.

Risk management choices in my island, Mauritius, depends a lot on incentives of different parties. Political personalities only focus on projects with short term benefits in order to gain support of the general population. The private sector companies are mainly concerned with their own profitability and individuals are often not aware of the importance and opportunity that lies in risk management. Therefore, I would add a feature in the game that allows users to make teams, with each user playing the role of a different party. Whilst the score of the team would depend on the collective decisions taken, the individual scores would be based on the impacts felt by that user. The main aim of this addition would be to show that risk management is beneficial to everyone if the overall benefit is taken into account rather than individual incentives.
