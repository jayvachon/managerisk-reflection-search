In the game of risk assessment the where 3 resources that had to be manage and balanced in a correct way in order to succeed: knowledge obtained through research that did not have an economical cost but was time consuming and on the other hand, insurance and protection at a high economical cost. Also a fourth variable had to be taken in to account during the game; uncertainty was present and contributed to build a scenario where risk management strategies had to be taken into consideration.

Research or knowledge, I found to be the most important asset in the game. Whereas, insurance was a fixed variable, with little options to it. As for protection, I think I would place it in a second place, after knowledge and before insurance as it would make a difference, but not as important as research.

My conclusion is that non-quantifiable variables are the ones that make a big difference and although they suppose some money (not in the game but they usually do in the real world) the cost clearly pays off and sometimes they can be simple things but very effective.

If I had to add one thing to the game it would be a behavioral obstacle, either overconfidence or short memory as often these are obstacles that make risk management go wrong.
