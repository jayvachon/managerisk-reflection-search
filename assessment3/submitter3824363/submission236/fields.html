1.
To be honest at first when I started, I started without doing the tutorial because normally I like to find out things my own way. But after a few tries and never reaching further than level 2 I did the tutorial to find out what I was missing. So after the tutorial I found out about the lifting, comet monitoring and the green puddles after a comet hit the planet. With this knowledge I switched my strategy from buying insurance towards lifting the place and clicking the green puddles. Additionally when a very destructive comet was nearing I bought precautionally some insurance.
Compared to my first rounds before the tutorial with just using insurance I became much more comfortable after the tutorial with my lifting and clicking strategy. Depending on the comet I lifted the place and combined with clicking the green puddles I then often ended up with no damage. So I would describe my last strategy as adaptation.

2.
As can be seen from question 1 I played the game several times.
For me I would say that changing my strategy from insurance towards adaptation using lifting and clicking made me more comfortable as it gave me more flexibility and I do not had to buy insurance for high premiums but rather invest the money in lifting and upgrades.
For reality I would say that the game is a great introduction but in reality I would prefer much more insurance than I used in the game. The background is that I think reality is much less predictable than the comets in the game and so insurance gives people a good protection against unforeseeable risks like "black swans" by spreading risks over a huge amount of people leading to little costs for each individual. Of course local precautionary measures are also important.
