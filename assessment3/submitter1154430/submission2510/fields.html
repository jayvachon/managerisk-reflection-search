What was the role of risk management in the game? What choices did you make and what trade-offs did you confront in terms of knowledge, protection, and insurance? In what ways did preparation for risk affect development (immediately, or after some time)?

 

Risk management was represented simply through the risk of destruction to pods and the measures that one could take in order to mitigate or avoid the risk of damage. One could gain a basic understanding of risk management by making choices about the level of research, protection and insurance one should invest time and money on. In the game, I tried to achieve a balance between insurance and protection; I protected my pods up to around four bars and insured up to around level two. However, I found that time spent on research was well spent, although this did leave me less time to build and upgrade pods, which meant that time often ran out before I could reach a certain level in the game. If several comets were coming at once, I was reluctant to invest more money in development until the risks subsided.

If you played the game only once, what would you do differently if you were to play it again? Alternatively, if you played the game more than once, what did you do differently after the first time? What were the main things you learned from the game?

 

I played the game several times in order to get up to level four. It took me a while to get a handle on the game – to get a good balance between research, insurance and protection – while developing pods in a limited period of time. It helped demonstrate how tricky risk management can be while you’re trying to weigh up the impact of the risks against cost of mitigating risk. I found that it was worthwhile buying insurance and protection, but realized that it was not worth buying too much of both, as together they help to reduce the damage incurred. Research and communication seemed a very effective approach to risk management.
