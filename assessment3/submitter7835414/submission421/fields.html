The game is set on the fictional island of Alora, where the main risk present is the risk of flood from a meteor impact. Various risk controls are available; including the ability to purchase insurance, increasing the elevation of the island improving is vulnerability and the ability to conduct research on incoming meteors. Risk management plays a role by balancing the development of the community while simultaneously managing the possibility of a negative consequence from the hazard. The main trade-off in the game is a trade-off between economic development and cost. Risk management costs community funds, which could alternatively be used to further develop the community. A decision is always made as to either purchase the risk control, or to upgrade pods to enhance economic development. This decision directly affects development, as the choice not to purchase a risk control will save money in the short term, but may cost in the event of a meteor impact.

The first time playing the game was done by purchasing the best insurance, using all plots of land to have pods and using all available funds to upgrade pods, and researching upcoming meteors when doing nothing else. Level 2 was reached during this play through. The decisions made in the next game were to only purchase insurance if a high risk (using risk is a function of probability and consequence) meteor was imminent. This saved funds in the first few levels, but saved even more in the later levels as insurance premiums rose. Research was always undertaken as it can be done without financial cost. However, it does take time and not possible to raise the land while researching. Land raising was only done when a high risk meteor was detected and the land had not already been raised. Therefore, it was not done anywhere near as often as research. Pods were upgraded as soon as funds were available. If I had the opportunity to do it again, I would make some insurance money available, so if a high risk meteor was detected, insurance could be purchased before impact.

In the development of a new game, a change that could be made could be that insurance becomes more expensive if a high risk meteor is approaching. This more accurately reflects real life situations, where insurance is more expensive if the insured items (in this case, the pods) are in a high risk environment. This better represents risk management choices in real life, for example motor vehicle insurance premiums are dependent on aspects such as age (where younger drivers pay more as they are inexperienced and have a higher probability of crashing) and where you park your vehicle (premiums are higher for those who park in the open as compared to in secure parking as the severity of event is higher). Therefore, as the risk changes, the insurance payment should reflect this. While there are many more factors, the probability and consequences that make up risk is the underlying basis of risk management in the real world, and the game makes an simplified reflection in a controlled setting of risk management.
