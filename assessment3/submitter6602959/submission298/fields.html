The game turned out to be harder than the interface looks. I have tried a total of 3 times, with the first 2 attempts failing at level 2.

Initially, I thought that the balance between knowledge, protection and insurance will be easy to achieve. I was wrong: During the first attempt I focused on insurance and protection, thinking that it will effectively fend off comet strikes and minimize potential losses. This way, I believed that a solid protection will lay the foundation for rapid development. However, the game was designed such that I could not accumulate knowledge fast enough at later levels. Though I used ample risk preparation, the effects were not immediate.

In my second attempt I focused more on gaining knowledge, using almost all the money to build infrastructure while preparing less and buys no insurance. It worked for level 1 and my growth almost exceeded the bar. However, when a huge comet strike I lost most of my infrastructure.

After the failure of the two previous attempts I have learnt that protection and insurance is indispensable, but at earlier levels it is more advisable to build up the base for rapid knowledge expansion, which is what I did in my last attempt. I managed to get to level 4 this time.

In a newer version, I would like to suggest providing more building types, players can now research in scientific buildings that provide rapid knowledge development but takes very long to build. Additionally, if it suffered damage from comets during construction, building progress will be lose. This is to introduce a high reward but high risk component to the game. These changes aptly represented how the forefathers of Singapore (where I live) took on much risk to develop its high tech industry that would later provide value-added service to the world. Those decisions has shaped Singapore into a world-class exporter of value-added products and transformed the economy into one of the most advanced in the region.
