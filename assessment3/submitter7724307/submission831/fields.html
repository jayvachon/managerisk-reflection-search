The role of risk management in the Risk Horizon game was extremely crucial. The game taught players how to balance the responsibility of developing the town while protecting it against risk, which in this case were comets. The game included all of the main components and key points that were discussed in the video lectures, such as obtaining knowledge by monitoring the comets, increasing protection by raising the town and minimizing damage, and buying insurance when there is adequate wealth to prepare for disaster. These were all very important to the survival in the game and moving on to the next level.

While playing the game there were definitely trade-offs and risky decisions made. For example, the player had to monitor the speed and the likeliness of the comets making contact with the planet. The comet research and pod development takes time and it definitely forced the player to manage the time clock efficiently while trying to balance the two tasks. In addition, there were trade-offs with the amount of wealth the town had. The player had to decide between using the money to upgrade pods and reaching the development goal or upgrading the insurance package to protect it from comets. The preparation for risk took more time and a larger sum of money, which sometimes affected the amount of development that could have been completed. This taught the player to accumulate and save money when possible, so it can be used when a risk does occur.

I played the game once and to my dismay I only reached up to level 5 out of 6.  If I were to play it again, the first step I would have taken would be to raise the town immediately to level 3 and start the pod developments right away. The raised town is a good prevention method while the pods are being developed. While development is occurring, the player is allowed to do research on the comets and take even more preventative measures. I believe this would have been more effective if I played the game once more. If this were to happen in real life, institutions and the government of the country would learn from this experience and hopefully improve their risk management for the future.

If I were tasked with developing a new version of the game, I would add the addition of coping strategies besides the use of money to repair damages. If the planet were larger, I would include evacuation plans and encourage the player to create their own and update evacuation plans based on the damage caused by the comets. Similar to how the insurance plan would have to be renewed each level, the coping strategies would also have to be updated. This was a great game because it taught players on a basic level how risk management is important to development. The game gave me anxiety because I had to think quickly on my feet, which is exactly how real life should be as well. Developers and locals need to implement preventative measures as well as react quickly when risk occurs.

I currently live in Washington D.C. and I would propose to change where and how energy is being obtained. If our energy could be switched to sustainable solar or wind power it would make this great city more resilient to the future of climate change and severe weather conditions that have been occurring all over the world. These events will only continue happening, and one of the first steps would be to change where we obtain our energy source.
