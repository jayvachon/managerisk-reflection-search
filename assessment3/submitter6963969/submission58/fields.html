In the game Risk Horizon the setting is somewhere in the galaxy on a undeveloped planet. Your role is to build habitations while the underlying risk of a meteor hitting the planet is always present. In order to continue developing the planet one must incorporate risk management by utilizing insurance, meteor research, and land elevation to deter the financial impact of the meteor impacts. By purchasing insurance the total financial impact of the meteors is offset by a percentage based on the level of insurance, however, this sacrifices available capital for development. By researching the meteor you learn about the severity and likelihood it will hit the planet, but you spend time that could be used otherwise. By raising the elevation of the land you lessen the severity of the impact, but you use money and time that could be used on development.

While playing the game multiple times I realized that applying the risk management techniques too little or too much would adversely affect my success while progressing through the game. If I applied no insurance I would be forced to pay for the entire cost of repair if a meteor stuck the planet. However, if I applied the greatest amount of insurance then I would not have enough money to develop fast enough for the time restriction. Eventually I realized that applying the lowest level of insurance and about half of the land elevation while doing a reasonable amount of research on all of the meteors would allow enough time, money, and security to continue development in order to reach the next level.
