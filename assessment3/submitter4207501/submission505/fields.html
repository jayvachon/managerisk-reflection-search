I would like to begin by saying that I thought the game was well-designed and effective at conveying some of the central concepts put forth in the course thus far. Thank you to the game designers for a job well done!

Four lessons from my experience playing the game stand out above the others and clearly demonstrate the role of risk management in the game. Though I write in general terms, I hope that the reader will draw parallels to his or her own experience playing the game.

First, the need for constant development cannot be understated. Without continuous development, progress is unattainable. A holistic approach is the most effective means of ensuring progress, and this makes sense intuitively. The Achilles’ heel of development, however, is access to wealth. Without a steady influx of wealth that increases with time, development can easily become stymied.

Second, an insurance plan that is optimized for your needs is a cost-effective means of safeguarding the gains that you have already made. Though it may seem like you are incurring additional risk by selecting an insurance plan that fails to provide complete coverage for future expenses, the reality is that the money you don’t spend on insurance is sometimes better invested in areas such as development or protection.

Third, building strong connections will save you in times of crisis. This is especially true when confronted with crises of severe magnitude and duration. Banding together during a crisis allows for a more efficient and equitable distribution of aid to the places where it can do the most good. Thorough institutional planning and robust networks between individual actors can facilitate an effective response to a crisis.

Fourth, determining your appropriate level of protection depends deeply on your level of knowledge. Knowledge is a function of research and experience. Good research will inform you about the types, severity, and frequency of hazards you are likely to face in the future. Experience helps to guide your research and steer policy in the right direction.

The first time I played the game, I failed to complete the first level because I did not place a greater emphasis on development and building connections. Even though my community did not sustain damage during the comet strikes, I focused too closely on purchasing the most expensive insurance and acquiring the highest levels of protection. This severely hindered my inflows of wealth and limited my capacity for development. The second time I played the game I adopted a more balanced approach and succeeded in completing all six levels.

The one change that I would make to the game would be to better clarify the link between the rate of wealth acquisition and level of development. While I assumed that developing my buildings in the game would lead to a more rapid influx of wealth, I felt that this was not explained during the tutorial as well as it could have been.
