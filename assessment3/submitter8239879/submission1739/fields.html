In the game we managed the risk of comets hitting the planet through protecting the pods, deciding how much insurance to take out, and if a comet hit then creating community links and rebuilding the pods. I found I made trade-offs between building more pods to create and help development, and buying protection, as both cost money. I also found myself buying more insurance and then using less protection. This was because it was a lot quicker and took less effort to insure my pods than it did to protect them. Preparing for risk had no affect at the time, but when a comet hit some time after, I was thankful for the insurance and protection as it meant that my pods could recover quicker from the disaster and at a lower cost. However, at the time of buying the insurance and creating the protection for the pods, development was not as quick due to the spending of money. This discouraged me from adding the highest rank of insurance or creating a lot of protection.

 

The second time I played I bought more protection for my existing pods as opposed to building more pods. This meant I could take better care of my current pods and they would recover quicker if a comet hit, as there were less to repair and community links could be created quicker between all the pods. I also bought a higher rank of insurance the second time round. This meant my pods could overcome the effects of a disaster quicker and at a cheaper cost than if I had bought less insurance. Finally, I learnt that when more community links were made straight after a disaster had struck, the less overall damage there was to the community and the better it coped with the disaster.

 

If I had the opportunity to make a change to the game, I would first create more than one risk. I thought the idea of the same type of risk having different severities (i.e. the comet) was very clever, but in the real World there are lots of different risks that you need different approaches, different protection for and not all of them you can obtain insurance for. Secondly I would add an opportunity type of risk. This would be whether or not to take a risk on, that may or may not end up with a positive outcome. For instance, if a pod was given the opportunity to relocate to a different part of the planet that may or may not have a more diverse and higher food supply, increasing the pods food intake and their potential for building more pods and increasing development.
