The game was an excellent demonstration of the trade-offs and inter-dependencies between the three components of risk management. I found that early preparation for risk allowed development to proceed more reliably in later levels.

I tried to get development going mildly at first, then invest in protection and research, and then focus on development more aggressively.  I learnt that insurance was not worth the expense especially at higher levels of coverage. The game gave a clue at level 4 that protection expense has diminishing returns, which was helpful. The main thing I learnt from the game is the temptation of skimping on protection and research only to lead to the pain of seeing a lot of damage done by the comet.

Instead of being a timed game, I would give more quantitative information on the trade-offs and allow for more time to make decisions before exposing the island to a series of continuous meteoric outcomes. This approach would better simulate how risk management proceeds in the real world. As a note to the educational component of the game, this change would also be helpful to allow the student to simulate more scenarios and understand the trade-offs faster. Currently after I lost a level, I had to go all the way back to build up from level 1 again.
