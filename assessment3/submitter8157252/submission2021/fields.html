I attempted this game more than once because I wanted to try different strategies.

First, I tried simply to protect from waves the pods I had built. I wanted to verify if it was possible to develop the pods only by preventing them from being damaged. It was not the case, because the remaining resources and money were no more enough to allow a development within the given time. Therefore, in another attempt I decided to allocate some money for insurance. I decided for an insurance plan with a 50% coverage. I think it was the right choice because it allowed me to invest more money for pod development. I paid also more attention to the information about the comets (chance of the comet hitting, and the severity of the comet). With this information I protected in a more effective way the pods, without wasting useful resources. In a third attempt I purchased a cheaper insurance plan. That was a good strategy for small damages, but when the damages were huge, the coverage was too small and the repairing costs too high, so that they prevent pods from achieving a good development in time. I tried also another strategy: I insure pods with a 75% coverage plan to recover lost development time and I used the rest of resources to develop the pods. This strategy proves as well to be not winning. Despite the very high insurance coverage, the damages caused by some comets were so severe, that the time and costs to repair them were too high. Some of these costs could have been avoid just by observing comets and protecting pods. That has clearly shown how the different components of the risk management are all needed.

I have found this game a very pleasant way to test the idea of effective and responsible risk management consisting in a balance between developing and preparing for potential shocks. But I have found the time at disposal to complete each level too short. Sometimes I took a choice too quickly, without having the time to think about a better option.
