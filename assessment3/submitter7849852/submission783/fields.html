(Q1)       The role of risk management in the game was related with the decisions concerning the balance between Risk preparation and Developing your community. In this frame one had to choose where to spend the valuable limited quantities of money yet all possible choices were related to risk management. The available money was allocated between:

  • Research: Knowledge about hazards (comets)

  • Protection: Decline the impact of a catastrophic event

  • Insurance: Mitigation of the consequences

  • Development: Increase your productivity and growth

During the game I spent a great amount of money in research and protection and a small amount for insurances. I made that choice because of two reasons. The first reason was that protection and research were more efficient and cheaper when a comet hit, giving more time to overcome the catastrophic event ,without spending a great amount of money and with a small impact on development in the long term. The second reason was related with the fact that insurance was very expensive and after a catastrophic event the development was notably affected either in the long term or in the short.

All the above mentioned depict that the most crucial policy-making decision, about preparation for risk, was how to protect from a catastrophe and this decision affected the development and the cost.

(Q2)       I played the game more than once and I change my strategy on three things. First, I spent less money for insurances because the damages affected the development. Second I increase the money for protection and research. By this way, I was able to reduce the consequences. Finally, I developed my pods in the same level because in that way the damage was smaller.

In general the game taught me that prevention for a catastrophic event is better from acquiring damages after it, triggering my interest about current programs from catastrophic events in developing countries.

(Q3)       Despite the fact that the financial crisis had an impact on all European countries, my country Greece was in the worst position of all. I mentioned the latter in order to propose a change in the mechanism that a hit from a comet affect the pods. In an effort to encapsulate the global nature of the shocks, except from national, it would be better if each pod or some clusters of pods were influenced in different ways. I suggest this change in the game so as to be more understandable that an international cooperation is necessary when national resources are not sufficient to cope with a catastrophe.
