I enjoyed playing the game but certainly found it frustrating at times! It took a few rounds to figure out some strategies that would work the best. I still didn’t find the best strategies, however, as I couldn’t get past level 3 in the 30 minutes or so I spent playing it.

Initially, I tried doing as much of everything that I could. As soon as I had money I would build or buy insurance, etc. However, after a few rounds I found that often the best strategy was to wait for a little while. Also, instead of trying to build as furiously or raise the island as much as possible, sometimes watching the comet for as long as possible was the best course of action as it maximised the time for creating community connections and that was important when there wasn’t enough time to try and protect before the comet hit.

If I were to play again, I would focus on determining exactly what worked when so that I could do better in the next round by employing what I learned. I found that initially, I didn’t really pay attention to exactly what I did and I think this would make a great difference in the future.

One option that may need to be employed for this game (and something that some island nations are needing to discuss) would be the option of leaving entirely. Given the shocks in this game are entirely non-preventable, there may be a need to relocate in order to ensure the viable economic future of the people or else they will all spend their entire lives just dealing with shocks. Although fictional, this game exemplifies the fact that the area is unable to pursue opportunities because they are always dealing with the effects of shocks. Due to the effects of climate change, some small island nations are facing this very dilemma – do we secure our future by leaving our home or put all of our resources on trying to protect the home even though we have no control over the shocks.

Overall, I enjoyed the game and think it is a great concept. It would be nice to have opportunity woven into it to really demonstrate the essence of this course, but perhaps that will come at another time!
