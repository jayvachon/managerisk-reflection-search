The role of risk management in the game was clearly to build resilience by mitigating the severity of negative shocks and to enhance the ability to cope with the after effects of adverse shock, fostering quick recovery. It also played the role of achieving prosperity by improving development through undertaking risk coated opportunities for prosperity/development. All of these involved getting insurance and the necessary quantum of it, engaging in research with respect to the expected or envisaged shock as well as elevating the area mapped out for development as a protective strategy.

Different choices and trade-offs were necessary in the process of playing the game. For instance you had to sometimes make a choice between what insurance to purchase at what time, when to elevate (protect) at the risk of loosing the game as the side bar turns brown and then red upon attempting to elevate; choices also of the pursuit of research with regards to the approaching comets, which research also had the effect of reducing or sometimes eliminating the severity of the damage; there was also the choice of applying available funds to developing the pods, creating new ones, repairs and further insurance. Sometimes the development process was sacrificed for research and continued research to mitigate the severity of impact, at some other times repairs were expedient and inevitable just as continuing the development of the community was and at other times it became necessary to hold up the key to elevate the platform in order to reduce the impact of the shocks. In essence, insurance was always necessary while the whole development project was underway and as it became expedient from level to level the insurance cover had to be increased. Knowledge was key and protection necessary. But knowledge ahead of protection reduced the severity of the risk faced while it also allowed for time to increase funds necessary for both development (including repairs) and buying insurance cover.

Preparation for risk either completely eliminated the risk or greatly mitigated its impact. However, preparation for risk limited the time available for development but gave the opportunity to accumulate the funds needed for further development.

Playing the game more than once, I learnt to take my insurance early enough otherwise the insurers were at some point of imminent danger not ready to insure even though you had the fund for the insurance cover. Also learnt the increasing the spate of development was necessary as it increased also the availability of funds just like research and that early insurance resulted in low cost repairs. Research was expedient always and as aforesaid reduced the severity of the shock and under certain circumstances eliminated it; protection created some risk of losing the game if one was not careful but if you got the timing right it was good. I observed also that the cost of repairing severe damages was on the high side and without insurance it was exceptionally high. The cost of development was as well high especially when you had to improve on the pods.

On question number 3, the need to include the ability to engage in several risks management options at the same time perhaps with delegation strategies.
