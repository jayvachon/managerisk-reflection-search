The game was a well-constructed hypothetical situation of risk management; preparing before shocks and coping afterward, highlighting its importance as an essential tool for development. Alongside building and upgrading the pods that would increase the rate of development, represented by the development bar on the left, there was a need to watch out for comets that were what we call shocks in reality. This accounts for obstacles and constraints that stand in the way of responsible and effective risk management, and tackling them. In an attempt to keep a balance between development and preparing for risks, I had to distinguish between three different risk management activities; research, also known as knowledge in reality, protection and insurance. Using the power of research by keeping the comet’s box focused on the screen for as long as I could, I acquired as much knowledge as I could about the approaching comet (shock), assessing its severity and chances of hit. In the first few attempts of the game I would spend only a two or three bars on research, however after a couple of tries, I realised prolonging the time spent on research had a positive effect, reducing the impact on the pods as I attained more time to connect to communities just before the comet would hit. However, the challenge I faced was that the more time I spent on research, the more I was compromising on the time that could be spent on protection. Although in the first few tries of the game, I did spend relatively more time on protection, in the latter few tries, I spent more time on research as mentioned above. Given a number of insurance plans to choose from, what I had to consider was the amount of resources available at hand. Torn between choosing an effective insurance plan and protection that was draining resources, I would choose to employ the insurance plan that may have been the most expensive at times but in the long-term it turned out to be the correct decision, as it reduced the cost of repairs for the damage caused by the comets. This signifies the importance of long-term planning. At times, due to shortage of resources, I had to choose the cheapest insurance plan in the beginning, but as I gained resources by developing and upgrading the pods, I would change my insurance plan to a new and improved one. And on the other hand, at times due to a huge chunk of my resources being spent on the insurance plan, there was a lack of resources, limiting my ability to upgrade the pods, thus slowing the pace of development of the community. I learned that risk preparation is highly correlated with income, and most importantly there is a need of careful planning and management to balance the time and resources available in a framework of obstacles, by thinking more strategically about the return over a long period of time. Moreover, to better represent the risk management choices that matter, I would propose to add another aspect of risk management that deals with a positive change such as technological improvement or a resource boom, and an indication of how to take advantage of it, clearly indicating the significance of developing upon new opportunities alongside threats.
