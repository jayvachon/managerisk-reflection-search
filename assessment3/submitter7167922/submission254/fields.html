The role of risk management in the game was to evaluate the risk, compensate for the risk, and balance those needs with the needs of growing the planet. I made choices to purchase at least the base insurance choice for each level, and on one level purchased the second choice of insurance. This offset my cost of repairs after damaging events. I always increased the height of the planet to avoid flooding and damage after comets. I chose the height based on the predicted severity of the oncoming comet. This hindered my ability to grow the planet very quickly; however by having those measures in place to compensate for the risk, I was able to continue growing the planet even though damaging events occurred. In essence, by using these risk management tools, I was able to avoid total collapse and rebuild and grow even more after a damaging event. If I had not made the risk management choices I did, I would have had higher rebuild costs that would have prevented me from building new pods and enhancing the existing pods. Those higher rebuild costs would have been prohibitive to growth. Therefore, by using accurate and successful risk management choices, I was able to achieve sustainable growth.

I played the game only once. I passed levels 1-4, and I did not pass level 5. If I were to play the game over again, I would increase the amount of insurance purchased for level 5. I only purchased the lowest choice of insurance, and would have been better protected during a damaging event, and therefore had lower post-event costs. The higher post-event costs forced me to rebuild slower and therefore not reach my goal in time. The main thing that I learned from this is that insurance is of utmost importance, especially the larger your investment and potential rebuild costs. Additionally, I would have added new pods earlier in the game. I only added a few initially and continued to enhance them, without taking advantage of early growth. This did not hinder me greatly in the long run, but would have made the last level or two a bit easier to continue growth with less stress.

To better represent the risk management choices that matter where I live, I would enhance the “research” feature of the comets, allowing better prediction of damaging events so that risk management choices could be better made. I would also have it estimate the cost of rebuild based on your current landscape and the damage that has been predicted.
