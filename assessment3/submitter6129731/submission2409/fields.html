I think this game gave quite a lot of insight into risk management. It is often quite easy especially when looking at policy makes to forget that there are hard decisions to be made. We might question why there is little or no focus on protecting a city in a tsunami prone area for instance but perhaps with limited resources it is better to focus on developing infrastructure which will in turn lead to an increased income that will enable the city to invest more heavily in protection.

The trade-off that I found I had to make most often was between protecting my community and developing it. On the one hand if I focused on simply developing the community without protecting it I found that I would get damaged more often and the impacts of the strike would be severe thereby negating any development that had taken place. On the other hand if I chose to focus on protecting my community a lot of the time I would end up losing the level as I would not have met development targets. If however I was able to protect the community say up to 4 or 5 bars, I found that it was much easier to then manage development, research and insurance. As long as the community was protected and I had insurance I could develop fairly easily spending more time researching comets with a high severity level and ignoring those with a low threat.

The game forces you to think about trade-offs and what is more important or more of a priority at each point. Another important aspect is that it shows you that there is no correct formula. There is trial and error involved in risk management and no one strategy works for everyone; you simply have to find the strategy that works best for your situation. An example of this is that I found whilst playing the game at the early levels it was more beneficial to focus on insurance and developing three or four pods very quickly and spending time on research when a particularly huge comet was going to hit. In this way I was able to reach the development level required quite quickly then stop developing and focus entirely on protection until time ran out. This meant when I got to level 2 I would have at least 3 or 4 bars of protection to begin with. As insurance got more expensive however I tended to wait until a particularly big comet was coming before I bought it and rather focus on developing and protecting simultaneously with a lower focus on research.

An interesting addition to the game could be an environmental aspect, for example an environmental bar that starts full and reduces as development increases and perhaps if the environmental bar gets too low it begins to hinder development in some way. I think this is an important aspect because whilst there are many risks to development that need to be managed there are also many risks/threats caused by development that need to be managed as well.
