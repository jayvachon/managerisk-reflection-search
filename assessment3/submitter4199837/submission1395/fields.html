Playing the game was, in fact, a wonderful learning experience. It was quite like a hands-on-approach to learning. I got so hooked to the game that I played it several times over.

At any rate, what I learnt from playing this game was that development work must begin in the earnest. Though simple development work is not all, it is wise to buy maximum insurance at the very outset, when resources are more fully at one’s disposal, in anticipation of a possible threat. As soon as insurance has been bought, it is time to focus on putting in place mechanisms of protection to prevent devastating losses.

It is advisable to being to observe threat, and conduct research on it, as soon as it emerges. Based on your research, you should assess how much protection you need. Unusual amount of attention paid to protection mechanisms can seriously stall your development work, hamper growth, curtail important resources, and even take attention off from threat analysis. Once sufficiently secured, you should focus on upgrading your physical structures, bringing in protection through adapting your structures to be better be able to cope when a disaster hits. All the while research on, and observation of, possible threats must continue, because it allows a bettered response, and you are better prepared to deliver it. Response to disasters is best done through linking the community and making it act together, and in cooperation.

Too much expenditure of resources, and expending them too quickly also sets you back. You should conserve resources to things like buying more insurance or repairing and instituting relief efforts after a disaster strikes. You should start new development work only once physical structures already in place are sufficiently upgraded and secured, to minimize losses. This does not mean that you do not buy insurance for new development projects. Buy insurance for all new physical infrastructure, and otherwise, as soon as threats emerge and your research tells you odds of you being high are high. Conversely though, If statistical data predicts that chances of a threat actually making an impact on development are very low - low to the point of insignificance – it is okay to ignore such threats, and focus instead on developing risk management mechanisms which will prepare you for a more formidable threat.

 You need to continuously upgrade your protection in light of new analyses of severity of threats as may be posed, or as may be emerging. A disaster that is more severe than you are protected against or have insurance for, will leave a far larger trail of destruction. Similarly, if you realize through your research that the severity of any potential disaster would be very high, it is advisable to stop all development, upgradation or similar work, and focus on developing protection, and once the disaster hits, to move as quickly as possible, covering as much ground as possible, to link communities up and form a joint response mechanism, because some threats are too severe to be fully protected against. Mitigation, thus, becomes key.

All this, and more, I have learnt through playing this game. As you can see, I made much of the game. I think the learning from the game confirmed, in many ways, what we have learnt through lectures and our readings. I feel that the singularity of the threats posed is something of a downside to the game. Perhaps, if we get a wider variety of threats, with different protection, insurance and coping mechanisms, it would make the experience truly enjoyable, and enhance the players’ learning manifold.
