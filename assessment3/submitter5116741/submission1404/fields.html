REFLECTIONS ON GAME

The role of risk management in the game was not any different from what it is in real life i.e. to minimize the damage to existing development and continue pursuing development with the limited resources at our disposal in the face of impending natural calamities (e.g. cyclones, earthquakes, draughts, tsunamis) and other disasters like world financial meltdown, nuclear disaster, war related migrations and evacuations etc. These real life phenomena have been represented by striking comets in the game.

In terms of knowledge, protection and insurance the choices to be made were getting information through research regarding the probability and severity of a disaster (as in case of game, the chances of a comet hitting the island and thus destroying the development with varying degrees of severity occurring, moving the island to a safer height and buying insurance so that the resources are not fully depleted in reconstruction and something is saved for further development). Ultimately comparing the cost-effectiveness of preparing for risk with that of coping with its consequences is an important trade- off. Preparation for risk becomes more important once some development has been achieved in order to protect that development and progress further. Otherwise if we don’t prepare for predicted risk we may lose whatever development we have achieved so far, then going back to zero will make it very difficult to cope with.

I played the game a couple of times trying to utilize the limited resources for development by calculating risks, setting priorities and making choices. I don’t think there are different ways to do it. Only thing I feel is that I was not very focused and the game factor i.e. the time is running-out thing didn’t let me do it calmly and focus on the real components of risk management.

No doubt, the game captures in a very simplified way the four components of risk management i.e. knowledge, protection, insurance and coping, but I feel the time horizon component in real life problems is different. From research we try to predict when a disaster is likely to occur along with its severity and probability of occurring. That avoids any panicking to creep in the risk management system on this account. Once we know the amount of time we have for preparation against an impending disaster along with the knowledge of resources for preparation it is easy to do it more calmly. So I would add in the knowledge component that when a comet is likely to strike along with the given components of severity and probability to make it more close to a real situation.
