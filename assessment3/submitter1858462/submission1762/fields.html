Risk management is a discipline where several actors play crucial roles with a lot of interdependencies. The game proposed in Week 2 is a pedagogical tool to understand all this actors in a fun environment. Humanity progress has been defined by growth of populations and their conditions: in the game symbolized by the number of pods and level of complexity. However, success in development requires not only focus resources in the progress of communities but also a proper risk management of different types of shocks which naturally occur: in the game symbolized by meteors with different level of intensity and probability to impact in the community.

In order to manage threads, risk management provide some tools like knowledge, represented by research in meteors flight, protection against floods in the game by rising land, insurance of damages in case they happened and coping with reconstruction when needed.

The trade offs between all the alternatives in which the player (decision maker) may act are interesting:

  • When to decide to invest in community growing?
  • Why to research in meteors and not protect the land?
  • Once the shock happens, is it worthwhile to rebuilt directly every pod or is it better to connect communities?
  • ...

My personal strategy has been always spend the resources in growth while the level of thread was low; simultaneously invest in insurances step by step. Once meteors appear in the sky I reduced the internal investment and I research in meteors with high intensity while increase the level of protection rising the ground. Risk preparation has some delay always respect to necessity, and this fact was damped with progressing insurance policy established in the long term.

The game need to by played several times, at least in my personal case. My understanding of this fact, apart of my personal ability with video games, is that in risk policy, experience is important, but also intelligent feedback from the past: communities and politicians with capacity of learning from the past will be much more effective in risk management. Another important issue that I learned from the game is the need of considering all factors at the same time. The optimum result of the game requires from integrated strategies in every relevant aspect:

  • Without investment in development it is difficult to collect benefits to prepare the population to risks.
  • Research is crucial to gain knowledge of the risk you may need to manage.
  • Insurances is a good way to delay spending of money between prosperity to crises.

A strategy conducted only in one direction is very likely to fail. Only common strategies developed with long term policies and fast application will succeded.

I live in a prosper country of the OCDE where risk management has improved significantly during last decades regarding natural shocks like floods or earthquakes. However, threads due to human action like ecological disasters of financial crises are not so properly managed. My proposal for the game will be to include somehow the effects of crises emerging directly from community actions. In particular, financial oscillations in markets may affect the level of development significantly and I am sure decision – makers need to establish tools to minimize these effects on people: how ? This is still an open question that many people wonders right now, but for sure with the use of knowledge, protection, insurances and coping with consequences.
