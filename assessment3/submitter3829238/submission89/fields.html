After trying a few times I reached level 6 but always lost playing that level.  I learned that in the first few levels insurance is highly overrated. I only used it in level 5 en 6 when I could see some severe comets coming. Lesson number one: only use insurance when you know you need it or don’t have the money to repair your community in case of bad luck. Research was very important and once I found out the advantages (especially the time you gain for making community ties and thereby raising the ground level) it was my primary line of defense. Which brings me to my second lesson learned from the game: community ties made all the difference. Working together yielded much more protection than just raising the ground level by paying for it. My other line of defense was raising the ground level ( I am Dutch it is what we do at home as well ). The only problem was the raising of the ground level basically stalled your development. I only used it in the beginning when the pods are developing ( When they are repaired of upgraded the development is stalled as well) or at the end of each level when I had reached the pass mark already. I therefore upgraded the pods only at the end of the level unless I could early on see I would not make the development mark without additional development. This actually reminded me of the Keynes model of economics. Save money when times are good so you can spend it when times are bad. I would save the money as long as the development was in the green and then invest it in upgrading pods or upgrading protection just before times became rough again ( the beginning of the next level).

If I could change something about the game I would make the role of insurance more important. As said before I live in the Netherlands and we are one of the most insured countries on the planet ( as in people are practically insured for anything from health care to what if someone takes you to court and you can’t afford the lawyer fees). In real life it is not just one thing you are at risk from it is usually a few things. I would add another risk factor to make it more complicated.
