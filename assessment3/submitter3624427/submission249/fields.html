Having played the game a total of 4 times to reach level 5 I can say that Risk Management played a very big role in the game, as without using it early on I wouldn't pass level one.

But it did come at a cost as the resources were limited and both the Insurance and Protection, as well as upgrading the community, came at a greater cost with each new level.

Also, if I wanted to beat the game I had to reserve enough resources to make the necessary upgrades to the community to keep the bar colour green.

Each time I had to start over I played around with the resources provided and the risk management process to try to achieve the perfect balance.

If I tried to focus on saving resources to develop the village and neglected to start the risk management process by buying insurance, raising the platform to protect the village or forget everything else and watch the comets, it will mean failure once a fast comet comes in before I have time set up some risk management measures.

I learned as I played the game to always keep a reserve of money for the risk management process.

The first thing that had to be done once a basic village was being built was raising the platform, and buying insurance to protect against future “shocks” that came once a comet hits. I then had to balance between building and / or upgrading the houses in the village with monitoring the comets as they flew by.

That proved tricky when more than one comet flew by with different speeds. In the end I found that the maximum bars I could get through monitoring will only give me 35 seconds of time to create community connections to help boost my protection. That I found through trial and error could be achieved by starting the research once the comet was almost at the middle of the horizon till right before the comet hits.

With regards to insurance, a 50% insurance plan was in most times convenient as I estimated that the comets would hit about 50% of the time.

Also raising the village till 6 bars was enough most of the times provided that the research done was enough to get you at least 30 seconds of community connections.

But all this still wasn’t enough to get me to level 6! It just shows how hard it is to balance resources with risk management in real life.

One thing I would change though is to incorporate the accumulation of knowledge into the game form previous “shocks” by making it gradually cheaper to implement risk management measures if you are successful in averting the comets with zero damage.

This way it becomes even closer to real life situations where people learn from previous experiences and in so doing can do things at a lower cost.

I hope this was useful.
