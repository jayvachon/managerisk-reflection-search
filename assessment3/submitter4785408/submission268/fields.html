I think this game was very y interesting due to the fact that there was only one knows threat and at time it still was hard to manage risk. Imagine if there were multiple such as the real world; that’s why it takes decades to strike a risk management balance.

There were definitely tradeoffs in the three categories, depending on how full my tube was, if it was green, and how much time I had left on the clock. If I was at the half way mark I did more research, if time was running low I bought insurance, and I did not worry about protection to much because it took too long to rise. But when development was at its highest, it took a long time to rebuild once disaster struck.

After about the third time playing the game, I realized that I could not implement all tactics to protect development. Timing was and is the key to everything. Of course you spend more time focusing on one thing more than others, but that one task that was neglected must be implemented at the right time and to its fullest capacity.
