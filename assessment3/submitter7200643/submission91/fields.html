The role of risk management in this game was the trade-off between building up the infrastructure in the country and coping with risk. Coping with risk could be done in three ways, either by insurance, by protecting against the meteorites or by researching (i.e. knowledge).  I focused on the insurance and protection part, however, when spending money on these two risk-management strategies you could not build up the infrastructure as fast as you would like to. And if I understood the game correctly, getting the buildings to at least level 3 gave them a lot of protection in themselves. Preparation for risked made the development slower. It might have been better to focus more on insurance (and maybe knowledge) instead of spending a lot of money on the protection part. However, all three of them would be needed.

If I were to play the game again, I would focus more on research and less on protection (even though I would use both of them). In the beginning of the game I believe that is important to get some protection in order to avoid large damages on the buildings. When having built all the buildings, I believe it is most important to focus on the insurance part, so that if the protection measure does not work you do not need to pay for full repair damages. Finally, when you are building more robust buildings (i.e. going to level 2 and 3 of the building – I did not get any further), I believe it is more important to focus on the research part in order gain more knowledge about these meteorites.

In this game I learned more about the trade-offs in risk management, and in particular that when you have a limited budget it is not possible to invest in all measures and at the same time have a quick development.

I live in Norway one of the world’s richest countries. Considering individual risk, we have good insurances both privately and through the government (social security and so on). Furthermore, natural disasters are rare and if they happen (mainly great fires and hurricanes), the government are quick to help out in the area. If I would want to change the game, one way could be to make it more realistic maybe for a local farmer in a poor country. For instance, instead of just facing one risk (here meteorites), others could be included as well. In the example for a local farmer, this could be natural disasters (drought, fires, landslide, etc), illness, and so on.
