What was the role of risk management in the game? What choices did you make and what trade-offs did you confront in terms of knowledge, protection, and insurance? In what ways did preparation for risk affect development (immediately, or after some time)?

In the game, players confront risk when allocating resources between development, protection and insurance. Naturally, the immediate temptation is to focus the bulk of the funds on development (new plots and upgrading existing plots). On the opposite spectrum there is the urge to buy the most expensive insurance possible (covering 75%+) and elevate the land to four or five bars. The extra protection gives players a sense of security but this is at the cost of development. Often, settlements must forego preparation and insurance to benefit from development. Purchasing insurance or protection means less funds in the short-term. The opposite is also true: spending on more protection an insurance frees up time since players need to wait for funds to upgrade settlements. In both cases, this time could be used to improve research. Thus, my strategy involved taking either a cautious or optimistic risk strategy (either upgrade or build protection and insurance) initially and then customising that strategy based on knowledge. Hence, if the next few asteroids seems to have a low impact or low probability of hitting the planet, then I'd focus more on development. In cases involving high-impact, high probability scenarios, my strategy involved more protection while postponing development.

 

If you played the game only once, what would you do differently if you were to play it again? Alternatively, if you played the game more than once, what did you do differently after the first time? What were the main things you learned from the game?

I played the game multiple times. Looking back, I feel I played the game far too cautiously. Despite coming very close to level five, I realised that my emphasis on protection (high elevation), insurance, and knowledge generation was greater than my focus on development. In many cases, I would spend time focusing on knowledge so that I could construct a strategy that balances development and protection. However, I spent so much time accumulating knowledge that I didn't spend enough time upgrading the settlements fast enough. Similarly, I would spend precious time acquiring knowledge on asteroids with a low probability of hitting because I felt the uncertainty was too great. Instead, I think I should focus more on development and spend a less time focusing on insurance, protection and the accumulation of knowledge. This is especially true in the last stages of the game.


The game captures, in a simplified way, some aspects of risk management. Imagine you are tasked with developing a new version of the game, which allows you to change or add one simple thing. What change would you propose to better represent the risk management choices that matter in the place you live?

I would think an element that is missing in the game is the ability to migrate to other areas. The ability to migrate means that people do not have to take extraordinarily high risks in settling in any particular area. This flexibility also means that governments must work harder to ensure that their citizens are satisfied and choose to remain in their own cities. If citizens feel that the state or community efforts are lacking and leave as a result, the entire city/region/country will suffer. Therefore, governments will have an incentive to prevent economic stagnation and work together to ensure the correct levels of knowledge, preparation and insurance.
