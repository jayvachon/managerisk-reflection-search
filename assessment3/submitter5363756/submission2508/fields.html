The objective of the game was to meet the developmental goals, whose difficulty increased with increasing number of level. The role of risk management was central due to two reasons, firstly without it the developmental targets could not be achieved and secondly to prepare for the damage caused by falling comets. The game provided with three kinds of powers to the player, the power of protection, the power of research, and the power of insurance, each corresponding to protection, knowledge and insurance respectively. In the games I played I gave the utmost importance to research, the knowledge gathered through research helped me make decisions regarding risk management. Knowing the level of severity of the disaster along with the probability of the comet to hit the Alora allowed me to make decisions regarding funds allocation. Higher chance and severity indicated the need for more funds for recovery after the disaster. But by choosing research I did a trade-off on protection which in the later stages caused me to suffer due to high level of severity of hitting comets. While preparing for risks I realized that development in the shorter period was delayed but in the long term development was ensured through effective tackling of the risk.

 

If I were to play the game again I would prefer to spend more of my resources on protection and insurance. The rationale behind this choice will be that the long term costs that one has to bear in the absence of protection and insurance outweighs the cost one has to incur in buying insurance and developing protection measures. Keeping this in mind after the first time I gave more importance to protection and insurance. The lesson I learnt from this game is that risk management is all about maintain a delicate balance between the utilizing resources in all thematic areas of preparation that are  knowledge, protection and insurance, each with its own safety net against the damage caused by risks and the limitations to handle the crisis situation. So it helped me realize that risk management is a process in which one tries to avert the negative consequences through conscious decision making between various options, which sums up to be how we cope with the possible risk.  

 

Improvement on such a well developed game reflecting various aspects of risk management seems to be an arduous task.  The room for improvement is very small as the game addresses all the interlinked components of risk management. Given the situation in the place I live and the risks we face as a community I would like to add a component that would help develop the concept of shocks varying due internal and external conditions and with a variety of potential outcomes.
