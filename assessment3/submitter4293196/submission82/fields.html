1. The game looks at five fundamental things as a city is being developed – the risk of a comet strike; the severity of the strike, the cost of replacing/repairing buildings when a strike occurs, the ability to share the cost of repair throughout the community and the ability of the city to minimise risk through preventative measures. The threat of a comet strike was high due to the number of comets so it was prudent to prioritise preventative measures like raising the city. Raising the city was a relatively cheap measure to protect against damage and offered a quicker economic recovery then insurance because damage was minimised even in the event of more severe strikes. Insurance helped cover the costs of restoration and recovery after more severe comet strikes but it had little benefit for ones with low to medium severity especially once prevention measures were in place. Importantly, the high cost of insurance significantly slowed the development of the city whereas preventative measures did not. Moderate research was helpful in determining the likelihood and severity of the comet strike but the sheer number of possible strikes suggested that preventative measures were prudent. Taken together a more developed city with good preventative measures to resist damage for the majority of comet strikes was in a better economic position to recover quickly and continue to develop than an unprepared highly insured city.

2. The game took a couple of goes to understand the value of balancing risk with the speed at which one developed the city. Continually improving preventative measures as the city developed appeared to give the best outcome.

3. One other potential variable could be the location of the city relative to the water’s edge as the key risk is flooding due to comet strikes, rather than the direct impact of a comet strike on the city. If the city is further from the water source then it will likely experience less significant damage in the event of a flood as the flood must travel further thereby losing power (think tsunami) and less developed land will have less value and will therefore be cheaper to repair in the event of flood damage.
