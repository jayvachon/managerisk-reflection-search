1.
Risk management in the game was about investing initially limited resources in development that could sustain comet hits and subsequent flooding from waves. The choices I faced were how much to spend on building communities (pods), on upgrading life quality, on insurance, and on structural mitigation versus how bad the impact of a comet may be. The trade-offs were: 1) spending resource on protection when a threat was detected and forecasted to be oncoming instead of spending resource on upgrade, and 2) spending resource on upgrade while there were no threats on the horizon.
2.
i played several games. I learned early that I mustn't let development of pods slide in favor of spending most resource on protection. I also learned that one should buy insurance with maximum coverage as soon as possible, as it was a cheaper than to wait when more upgrades were made.
3.
If an advanced version is possible, I would add public opinion as a layer of complexity, that one would not gain as much resources if people feel unhappy with their level of development, and also add public awareness programs as a form of protection to improve the public's understanding for why it is necessary to invest in protection.
