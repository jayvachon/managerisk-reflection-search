At first the game looked like like any other game with the ususal choices and 'lifes'. But with the benefit of the lectures, it was easier to appreciate the puzzles from the perspective of risk managemnt. The role of risk management was quickly evident in the game early on as I had at my first try attempted to absorb the risk of guess-timating wich of the comets was likely to be more catastrophic from its physical character like speed. The effects were like playing Russian roulette; I lost woefully. Thus my non-preparation jeopardized my community.


The role of research/knowledge in everyday life was really highlighted as when I traded real knowledge for gut-feeling, I almost always lost. Also my personal reliance in everyday life of protection was challenged as a cure all, especially when it had the effect of retarding development in the game. Thus opportunities for better growth/development were avoideded instead of taking necessary risk. Insurance seemed to play a greater role in allowing me do better than is my real life appreciation of the tool, previously I only saw insurance as a defencive tool, but the game made me realize it could also effectively be used as an 'offensive' tool..


I varied the amount of protection and insurance I used while always using the benefit of research/knowledge and this got me to the highest level I got, though I suspect I was still playing much too safe with too much protection rather than insurance. The effects of this game is to allow me see the reality of the real world application of risk management. Occasionally i was stunned as the game seemed to reflect a political life cycle for most democracies where keeping the community safe is not good enough but also concerted community growth and in a timely manner, equivalent to the four year terms of a lot of democratically elected governments.Â  I will give the game another go!!


If a tweak for adapting the game to my local reality were to be added, it would have to be literacy-education as a catalyst to the use of research/knowledge. There is quite a bit of research/knowledge in the environs but there is a real disconnect between that information and the belief in the generality of the populace as to the efficacy of that knowledge. So thus in this game the research would only be as effective as the 'educated' members of the community who consult together to mitigate the effects of the comets' waves.
