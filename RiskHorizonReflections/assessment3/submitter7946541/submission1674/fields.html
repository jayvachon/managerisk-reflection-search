Risk management takes a very important role in the game.  When the community is developing, no matter upgrading or building new pods, if the disaster comes, the community will suffer a lot of damage if there are no risk management measures taken, which are risk preparations, like research, protection, and insurance. Between knowledge, protection and insurance, if resources allow, I will spend more on knowledge, protection and insurance.  But the proportions on these three sectors depend on real situations of the organizations and the nature of risk (the possibility and the severity of the risk), which could be done by cost-effective analysis. Preparation for the risk affects development in different ways: in terms of period, there are short-term and long-term affects; in terms of the scope, there are internal and external affects. For example, if a developing country is managing environment risk well nowadays, it will affect the country’s long-term development and also other countries’ developments.

When I played the game first time, I am not familiar with rules and tools, so it becomes better when I played again after practice. When I played more than once, the big difference is that I gain more knowledge of the game and the risk, so I began to know trade-offs between severity and chances, risk and development, and thought more about coping with risks (how to utilize the insurance and community connections). The main thing I learned from the game is that risk management is a complicated process, including preparations before risks, facing risks and dealing with risks. Every procedure affects the final result of the development. 

If I could change the game, I will add “internal risks” to the game. So the game will get closer to real world. Actually, in the real world, we are facing a lot of risks, but we are using different ways to cope with external risks and internal risks. So if people could pay more attention on these two kinds of risks at the same time, it will help to achieve the goal of resilience and prosperity.
