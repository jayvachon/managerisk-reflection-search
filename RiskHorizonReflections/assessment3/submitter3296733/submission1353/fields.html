Question:What was the role of risk management in the game? What choices did you make and what trade-offs did you confront in terms of knowledge, protection, and insurance? In what ways did preparation for risk affect development (immediately, or after some time)?

 Answer:The role of Risk Management in the game was with respect to the chances of losses due to the meteor hits or the opportunity loss (of the money available with us initially) and the choices available with us to increase our revenue (by building or upgrading pods) or avoid losses (by researching on the comets, buy insurance or raise the protection level of the island).

Additionally the instructions and the tutorial were not very helpful to learn the game either and I had to learn the nuances of the game by playing (and losing) repeatedly. I did some reflection after one or two games and got some feel on the game concepts.

Initially I tried to build more pods (and did not have the time to upgrade them) and without either buying insurance or researching the comets just kept raising the protection level to save them from the comet related damages. I had to spend substantially to just save the pods and hence lost the game.

In the next try, I went in for building a few pods, upgrade them, buy insurance and spend some time in the risk research and spend a few for protection level increase. I still did get some damages but overall the game was won probably as I was balancing all (developing, upgrading and risk management using all 3 tools) in some kind of proportion.

 Question:If you played the game more than once, what did you do differently after the first time? What were the main things you learned from the game?

Answer: I did play the game many times, initially to learn the game by playing. However after learning how to play in the first game I focused only on one type of development activity and one type of risk management activity. After that I had tried combinations of different proportions of these activities. For example, instead of building pods all over, build a few and upgrade them. Instead of only working on increasing protection level, use research, insurance and protection level in different combinations.

I learnt that to win you have to invest in assets (build pods, upgrade them) that enable revenue flow. At the same time you need to also invest time and money to manage the risks to these assets, by a balanced combination of risk research, insurance and risk mitigation (raising protection level). However as the overall money and the time available to you also are not unlimited, you have to balance all these (investing in development activities as well as risk management activities).
