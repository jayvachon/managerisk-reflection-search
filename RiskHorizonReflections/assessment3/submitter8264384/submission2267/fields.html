There’s no doubt that risk management plays a critical role in Risk Horizon. It’s the key to development of Alora. Staying in the relatively dangerous condition, only effective risk management can protect Alora from humorous losses. For one thing, in terms of enriching the knowledge of risk management, I researched every comet at least 1 time and researched more dangerous comets even 3 or 4 times in order to have more accurate data to set protection. For another, I insist on purchasing the most expensive insurance at every level, for once the hit happened, it was able to offer help as much possible. What’s more, I continued to enhance the level of protection but not all the time. The preparation for risk is costly and will reduce the speed of development immediately, as we can see from the left side. However, it avoided more losses when we faced disasters and was significant if our thinking pattern was based in the long run.

 

   I played this game more than once and have accumulated some experience. It’s essential to keep a balance of development (economic growth) and safety. Establishing more dams can enlarge the scale of economy and provide more capitals for protecting. And enforcing the protection rapidly can ensure the development. I have obtained a lot from the game and the main thing I learned from Risk Horizon was how important the valid risk management was. Most efforts such as buying insurance, establishing the connections between each village, studying comets and so on were the investments in advanced. Thanks for these efforts can we gain more possibilities of avoiding more losses and pursuing opportunities.

 

   I’d like to add a simple but effective warning system in the new version. If I start the system before the hit of the comets, my Alora can be damaged less. The more I pay for the system, the larger region it can warn. The place I live has had a grave earthquake several years before and the casualties were amazing, for the lack of effective risk management. Unfortunately, we know the disaster may happen again in the future. Taking this into account, more shelters and architects of better quality have been built. Besides, more research resources have been put into predicting earthquake and providing better education of self-protection for children. Although we are likely to face the disaster again, I’m sure my city will have a brighter future.
