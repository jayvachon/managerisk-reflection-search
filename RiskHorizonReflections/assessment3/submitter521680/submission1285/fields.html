I would like to start by saying that Risk is a part of life. It is everywhere. It plays a very important role in everything we imaging, in everything we implement. Quite obviously, Risk Management plays an important role in the game Risk Horizon. In every decision we make in the game, be it in building the pods or upgrading them or buying the insurance or even in the protection levels, somehow we calculate the risk associated with it. The game quite brilliantly demonstrates the key four components of Risk Management in a lucid manner. In the game, the pods face a single shock which is caused by the falling meteors. First and foremost, gaining knowledge on the meteors is a prime concern. But this hinders the development activities. Insuring the pods is the first thing I did. But when a shock occurred, there was a considerable damage to the pods. Even though the insurance was paid, it would have been better if the protection was rigid.

I had to forego some development activities when concentrating on Knowledge component (researching on meteors). But this part of activity helped in coping with the damage done and thereby reducing the actual overall damage. In the further levels of the game, it has become a tad difficult task to develop the pods while focusing on researching, insuring and protecting. But, these components have helped in sustaining the damage, how big the shock/disaster may be.

           

As it can be seen from the image, despite the high severity, good levels of research and protection helped in reducing the damage drastically to 6%. And Insurance helped to an extent in rebuilding. At times, the damage was surprisingly 0%.

            I played the game twice! In my first attempt, I focused mainly on filling the development bar with building pods, upgrading them and insuring them. With this strategy, I couldn’t even clear the level 1. When I played the second time, I balanced on the key components of Risk Management. I focused not only on developing and upgrading the pods but also on researching the meteors and protecting the arena. I maintained certain balance among the activities leading to effective development.

The main things I learned from the game are managing risk entails opportunity to growth and development, balancing the resources plays an important role in Risk Management.

            If I were tasked with developing a new version of this game, firstly, I would say that this game captures the aspects of risk management in a simple and easy manner and refuse to propose any changes in it, but it would have been better if the game also displays the death poll of communities there. Like the damage aspect should be clearer, it should separately show the death poll and infrastructural damage. Besides this, I would propose no change to better represent the risk management choices.
