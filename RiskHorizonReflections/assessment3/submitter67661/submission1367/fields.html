1) What was the role of risk management in the game? What choices did you make and what trade-offs did you confront in terms of knowledge, protection, and insurance? In what ways did preparation for risk affect development (immediately, or after some time)?

The role of risk management in the game was to identify and lessen problems encountered when trying to build a community of houses.

The choices I made were to use all of the three forms of risk management sparingly but not focus on one in particular. For example I would take some (but not the full) insurance, protect by raising the island a few bars and spend a little time researching each comet. I adapted the strategy slightly for each level so in later levels I would buy more expensive insurance if the likelihood and severity of a comet hit were both high.

For me, preparation for risk affected development because I would often wait to see if a comet hit before deciding whether to build a new house. There was no point starting to build a house only to see it damaged 5 seconds later.

If I played the game again I would but more island-raising protection on the earlier stages of the game and use insurance only on later levels.

The main things I leant from the game: I learnt that risk management strategies change and evolve over time, often in response to a change in the threats faced. You must strike a balance between progress (e.g. building new houses) and protection but placing too much emphasis on either or these would be a mistake. For example, you cannot have 100% protection if you then have no money to build houses. Also spending all money on houses without any protection would also be counter-productive.
