Risk Horizon is a game of risk strategy and understanding how to protect and develop Alora, a world which is in the pathway of ever-present comets.  Inherently, the success of Alora depends entirely on the level of expertise in applying risk management tools of knowledge, protection and insurance. 

To succeed, the correct balance of insurance, pod building and land levels must be delicately taken into account with monetary funding as a key variable.  In the first few trials of the game, I relied heavily on insurance and research with less emphasis on raising the land level.  Not taking into account the money level resulted in a number of comet hits and a higher severity of damage.  Insurance did not protect the pods from flood damage and excessive dollars were required to repair the community. 

Changing strategy for subsequent games, I channeled the resources towards building/upgrading the pods and raising the land level and applying less importance to insurance.  As I progressed to levels 4, 5 and 6, it was evident this was the right balance to ensure that the comets would safely pass with minimal damage.   Buying insurance became a back-up strategy, offering a sense of security, rather than relying on it as a primary factor in the success of preservation.  Research played a secondary role in the ability to reach higher game levels.  With a less than 100% reliability factor, it was used as a knowledge tool offering a brief look at a comet’s power to build the strength of community connections.   

If I were to add one game element, it would be the ability to install a transparent dome around the community to protect it from floods.  It would be a temporary structure with a set time frame of fifteen seconds and a cost of three hundred ($300) dollars.  The dome would change colors, from green to yellow to red, signifying the elapsed time.  The decision to activate the dome would require a cost-benefit analysis prioritizing research, insurance and expected comet damage. 

This game represents a rudimentary exercise on how the tools of risk management are applied to a given task.  The goals are simple: protect, sustain and develop.  With many unknown obstacles, the decision process becomes complex with many resources required to effectively follow-through to ensure resilience and success.  Being aware of all possible risks, though not always entirely feasible, allows for proper risk management implementation through the use of the given tools.  Proper planning, adopting a proactive strategy and maintaining a proper balance are the essential building blocks that lead to a sustained and stable foundation.
