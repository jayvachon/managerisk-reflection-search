Lectures for the last two weeks provide the path to development which is paid with both risks and opportunities. In order to develop and seize opportunities it is necessary to confront and even take risks. In fact as they go about their daily lives, people face not one, but many types of risks. Some of it hits specific individuals such as the risk of unemployment, crime or disease, while others such as natural disasters and financial crisis are systemic and cut across groups of people. People often don’t have the resources or ability however to confront the risks they face. There may be burdened with obstacles and constraints. Some of them are related to lack of knowledge and information, to lack of resources or financial constraints, while others are the result of lack of access to markets and public services.  

Although people face different types of risk, a common risk management concept can be applied.  According to the lecture 2.2, there are four main interlinked components of any risk management:

·         Knowledge: To understand shocks, internal and external conditions and potential outcomes, thus reducing uncertainty.

·         Protection: To reduce the probability and size of losses and increase those benefits

·         Insurance: To transfer resources across people and over time, from good to bad states of nature

·         Coping: to recover from losses and make the most of benefits.

Risk Horizon game provides for experience to manage risk of natural disaster when comets hit the earth which destroys my community. In the game, knowledge is the first and foremost component that I have to know, when a comet is coming, about its severity, chance to hit and level of damage, and at the same times I have to know what to do to build the community. When I know about risks, I can protect my pods by raising the level of my community in order to reduce the severity if the comet hit the earth.  As I have limited capacity, protection is not always the best solution. I therefore need insurance to cover my bad times of damages.  Whenever the comet hits the earth, I have to cope with it to recover from the losses by connecting within the community quickly and repair the damages as soon as possible.

To play well in the game I need to think about the balance between protection (preparing for risk) and investing in building my community pods and insurance. And if any hit, coping with impacts of the risk (connecting with other within community) is necessary. For example, when I first played I put too much emphasis on insurance, and could not easily move from level 1. Then I realized it is not enough to rely on insurance after the fact. I also need to invest heavily in protection. I still could not move from level 2 and I finally learned that I did not cope with the impact of the risk well. I started connecting within community and repair the pods as soon as the comet hit the earth. I could move to the next levels with this.

My lesson learned from the game is that I keep the balance between risk preparation and development of my community. If I spend too much on building and upgrading pods, I will be exposed to comets when they hit. On the other hand, if I spend too much on protection, I will not have enough money to develop my community sufficiently. This is a balance policy makers have to consider all the time in spending limited resources on risk management or on other development priorities.
