The role of risk management in this game was to avoid loss, i.e., the possibility of loss, by confronting risk, preparing for it and coping with their effects (namely, by reconstructing the pods). 

Preparation for risk, involved, according to the game, a combination of actions to be taken in advance to the eventual materialization of loss. Firstly, by gaining knowledge about the comet, by looking out into space and learning the probability of the comet hitting, the severity of the comet’s damages if a hit is made and the time left before its impact. The existence of several comets at the same time shows than even the resources allocated to knowledge must be applied under a cost/potential-prevention-of-losses analysis. More knowledge may also mean more protection if such knowledge widens the most efficient way to deal with the materialization of the risk of loss. Secondly, preparation by acquiring protection against the waves caused by impact, though some kind of barrier. This protection reduces the probability and size of potential negative outcomes resulting from the hit and subsequent waves produced. Protection can be the result of infrastructure but also, as the game shows, of better knowledge of how to face adverse circumstances. Moreover, if resources are transferred  to insurance one can assume more risk, or build less protection in benefit of development measures. Thirdly, insurance helped reducing the negative outcomes resulting from the hit, cushioning any losses that protection could not prevent. As insurance transfers resources needed from development, it is better to apply them after gaining information regarding the real impact of a non-insured risk. Hence, more knowledge can save you resources in the sense you can better assess your risk and transfer the adequate resources to cover the excess risk regarding which you don’t have protection, and not more.

The exercise also entailed coping, which encompasses all actions taken after the hit of the comet. It consists in deploying the knowledge, protection and insurance during the preparation phase, as well as learning with the experience and use such experience for better future risk management (next comet).

The resources provided by the game are limited, so it was important to set priorities regarding the development itself (construct and upgrade hubs) and the preparation for risk, which also consumed resources. Hence, the main concern was to assess the cost-effectiveness of preparing for risk with that of coping with loss, and the impact of such loss in the development of Alora.

But risk management also implies to take into consideration no only the competition for resources while deciding between “growing” and a proper risk management strategy, and regarding the latter, the allocation of resources to knowledge, preparation and insurance, but also the synergies involving these three dimensions of the preparation for risk.

 While playing, main concern was to assess the cost-effectiveness of preparing for risk with that of coping with loss, and the impact of such loss in the development of Alora. When I played again tried to have a more balanced allocation of resources, i.e., a larger investment in rapid growth or development of Alora, which gave me the resources to a better risk management. 

Finally, in what concerns changes in the game, I would insert in the game a multistakeholder analysis of uncertain scenarios regarding the losses (which would be variable, existing several types of buildings - ranging from basic infrastructures to more developed ones - with different repair costs and with different “investment return paces”) emerging from the hit of the comet.
