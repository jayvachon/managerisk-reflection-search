Risk Horizon - Reflections

The Role of Risk Management

Within the game the objective of risk management is to maximise the development opportunities for the world of Alora by lessening the severity of damage done due to the inevitable comets. The role of risk management here is to make choices and invest in strategies that achieve this objective.

Choices & Trade-Offs

The main choice to be made is really how to best balance time and resources put directly into promoting development with those put into risk preparation. Focussing solely on just one of these aspects of the game would leave the community very vulnerable to damage and suffering.

My initial strategy was to focus on long-term protection and raising the level of the island. This was expensive and time consuming, particularly at higher level of protection that was more often than not unnecessary. This was however a much more valuable tool to manage less severe threats once resources were more readily available later in the game.

All three of the preparation tools were highly complementary. The use of insurance was expensive only relative to the level of existing development. I believe it was best used as a back-up in the event of a severe shock. Often, the lower levels of insurance were better value so more resources could be put into development.

Utilising research (knowledge) to reduce the threat turned out to be a very worthwhile investment as it was more about time than money. Building community links and resilience was quite effective particularly when the community was less developed and resources were very limited. This appears similar to the simple and effective strategy of cyclone warnings in Bangladesh mentioned in the lectures and WDR.

Adjusting the balance between the alternative options to best represent the characteristics of the threat was very important. Taking into account the severity and likelihood of each threat meant no more than the necessary amount of money was used for preparation, freeing up resources for development. There are always uncertainties and investing in protection for extreme and rare shocks in the game and real life may be uneconomic, particularly when there are few resources available.

The Effect of Preparation on Development & Lessons from Attempts

Preparation consisted of a combination of protection, insurance and research. All three strategies resulted in better outcomes than inaction. Protection and research were able to reduce the immediate damage done whilst insurance reduced the cost of repair. This was the case immediately and over the long term. Reducing the immediate and short-term damage done had an exponential effect on the long term level of development as it allowed resources to continue to go into development rather than repairs.

I attempted the game several times. The main adjustment I made to my strategy was to focus more on development which in turn provided more resources to invest in both preparation and further development. This appeared to create a self-perpetuating cycle which made the game objectives a lot easier to achieve and I think acts as a good lesson for real world development.

Game Improvement

My suggestion would be to remove the necessary target on each level and replace it with a list of high scores for that user. I believe that this would better reflect the aim of the game which is to teach the concepts and challenges of risk management for development and for people to understand the way the different tools can interact and complement each other. This way the user could hone their skills in a progressive way whilst improving their understanding of the subject.
