The game captures all the facets of risk management – knowledge, protection, insurance and then coping after the event of a shock. To be able to develop the community effectively, one has to adequately balance all parts of risk management, bearing in mind the constraints of time and resources.

For instance, purchasing expensive, but high coverage insurance would mean low payout on repairs after a shock but would also imply that there would be fewer resources available for developing pods which would in turn impede development. Also, raising the community to protect it against shocks consumes resources that could otherwise be available for growth and temporarily brings development to a halt. These are trade-offs that one has to manage effectively in the bid to achieve growth.

After playing the game a couple of times, I was able to learn the importance of insurance. Initially, I would purchase a low level of insurance, if at all, and focus more on protecting the community and researching the comets. I believed that would help in avoiding the shocks in the first place and not having to be faced with the challenges of coping with them. Eventually, I learnt the importance of insurance and had to incorporate it into my game plan. There are unavoidable events in real life that despite adequate preparations and measures taken to avert crisis, they still do occur. That is when insurance comes in handy.

If tasked with the responsibility of developing a new version of the game, I would ease the time constraints a bit. There were a couple of times that the development of the community had surpassed the mark required to proceed to the next level of civilization, but would experience a major shock that would make the community regress. Often times, the community would not recover from the shock before time runs out. I believe in real life situations, development is not based on a strict time limit. Countries and communities advanced on their own time. A good example is Brazil that is making efforts to become a world power and would not have to wait till 2050, for instance, to be declared one. When it is adjudged to have reached that status, it is addressed as a world power.  So in my version of the game, the player would be allowed to proceed to the next level of development as soon as he/she surpasses the required level. This would ensure he/she is not subjected to a shock that would undermine all his/her efforts and make him/her lose the game.

I am sure that calls for the argument that economic growth should be seen to be sustainable before a new status is declared. In that case, once the bar is surpassed, a new time limit ought to be set to test the sustainability of the growth the community has achieved. In the game, some comets struck just a few seconds to time which was not enough for it to recover before the game was declared over.

In conclusion, it is a well-developed game that tasks the mental capacity of the player concerning the principles of risk management. I was able to reinforce some of the lessons learnt so far during the course of the program.
