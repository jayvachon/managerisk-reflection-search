My reflections for the Risk Horizon Game:

The purpose of risk management is to enable the progress of an individual or an organization towards its goals & objectives in a direct, efficient and effective path. It is used by organizations as well as individuals to manage activities whose outcomes cannot be forecasted exactly & precisely, i.e. Risk Management consists of methods which are taken to reduce the risk of damage to physical assets, exposure to legal liability, or injury to employees or customers etc.

In the Risk Horizon Game, our job was to develop the community of the fictitious place of Alora by working as its luminator to the best of our ability which is predominantly exposed to the risk of falling comets of varying severity & frequency. In essence, this included analyzing the severity & frequency of the risk of falling comets & protecting the community with the help of three luminator  powers: Research, Protection & Insurance. For Risk Management to be truly effective in such a scenario, it needs to combine capacity to prepare in advance with the ability to cope afterwards. Preparation includes knowledge, getting information on risk (falling of comets), and then using that information to make judgements, obtaining protection (like increasing the level of land by using the toggle switch or by making upgrades to the pod), measures to influence both the impact of probability or shock, or obtaining insurance, measures to transfer resources from good to bad times or to those in need of bad times (pods destructed due to falling comets).

As all shocks cannot be prevented, so people living in the pods need to be able to cope with it afterwards. This could include updating knowledge, and then using whatever other protection and insurance arrangements are in place. It was also seen by me during the course of gameplay that the benefits of paying in advance (by way of insurance etc.) for preparation against any risk exceed the cost of paying damages afterwards, often multiple times. The initial results might be inconclusive as we had to pay for repairs to the extent of damage to the pods as a result of falling of comets but both protection, initially by way of making upgrades to the pods or raising the level of land as well as resorting to protection vide insurance resulted in low impact of overall risk being felt.

The main objective of this game & of the preceding week's overview was to let us know about various facets of the process of risk management: creation of risk possibilities, risk classification, category of risk – pure or speculative , as well as risk attitudes which can be of the following types – risk neutral, risk preferer or risk averter. We also learnt about the cost of risk which could be either direct or indirect, cost of handling risk as well as cost imposed by risk which depends upon following variables – risk control measures, uninsured losses & insurance.

Finally, if I’m tasked with developing a new version of the game, which allows me to change or add one simple thing in the gameplay, then that would be is to make a provision so that people who have the right incentives & the right information and who are living in the pods in the fictitious place of Arola, are allowed to relocate to another place in the event of disaster like falling of comets. Also apart from falling comets, I would have made further provisions to include more risks under the ambit of the gameplay.
