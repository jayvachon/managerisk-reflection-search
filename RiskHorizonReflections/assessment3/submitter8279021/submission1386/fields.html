Risk Horizon Game

The role of risk management in this game is to find a good balance between the building a community through affordable and optimal risk preparation. I was able to reach the fourth level by sequencing my activities; (i) building the communities, (ii) gaining knowledge about the comets, (iii) using the gathered money to take an insurance, (iv) safe money for repairs, (v) use the remaining time to heighten the community protection level. My main trade-off was between buying an insurance vs. gathering knowledge on the comet risk. That is, if a comet expected severity and impact was low I delayed the buying of insurance and invested in building the community. Gaining knowledge about the comet, choosing for the appropriate insurance level and heightening the community protections resulted in less community building activities.   

I’ve played the game 6 times in the conviction I would be able to get to level 4 and gain the 10% bonus. While getting increasingly frustrated about the time lost, I adjusted my risk management strategy by becoming more risk-taking and  delaying and moderating the insurance level.  I also stopped repairing in the last minute observing that these will not be finished, and used the last 30 seconds to heighten the community protection level. I learned that priorities have to be set between gaining knowledge, protection and insurance. As a rule of the thumb I limited my “Comet Research” till the expected severity level, and included the time factor in decisions on construction and repair activities 

 

The game has succeeded and capturing the main elements of influence of risk-management; risk knowledge, insurance and protection. If possible I would like to add an option allowing to personalize the game through the profiling players cultural and/or individual character traits; e.g. level of risk-taking, type of education, country. Doing so, would allow the player to tailor and experience the influence/effect. This addition would further increase the game realism by balancing economic/rational decision making with personal, social and cultural factors.
