Reflections from the Risk Horizon Game

Introduction

The present essay aims to present the author's reflections about the Risk Horizon game, which is part of the evaluations in the course 'Risk and Opportunity: Managing Risk for Development', offered by the World Bank in Coursera platform.

Concepts Definitions

Risks, also called the ‘known-unknowns’ (Lechler, Edington and Gao, 2012, p. 59) can be defined as factors whose occurrence might facilitate or hinder the achievement of any kind of activities or objectives. According to the Oxford Dictionaries (2014) risks might indicate the possibility that something unpleasant or unwelcome will happen, so it is important their proper management.

Risks are presents in almost all activities (World Development Report, 2014, p 53.), and from the point of view of development these can be classified into systemic (natural disasters or financial crisis) or Idiosyncratic (specific to Individuals or households) (p. 53). With the concepts presented is easy to see how important the correct identification, quantification, prioritization and development of a risk management strategy is important in order cope with all risks might exist. This process is referred to risk management (Kerzner, 2009; PMI, 2013) and according to Ramona (2011) is composed of two main activities: risk assessment/analysis and risk mitigation. With respect to risk assessment or analysis, this can be done in two ways based on qualitative (Qualitative description of risks) and quantitative (risk resource values, frequency of occurrence, etc.) elements of risks (Ramona, 2011; Rot, 2008).

Game Reflections

The Risk Horizon Game allowed the author of this report to experience interactively, a process of decision making under conditions of risk and uncertainty. Through a quantitative risk analysis as defined in Rot (2011) and Ramona (2008), it was possible to manage risk by balancing actions towards the sustainable development of Alora, and actions to prevent negative impacts of falling comets.

With this game it was possible to perceive in practice the importance of preparation in the analysis and management of risks. Analyzing the probability of impact severity, the chance of hit as well as studying the comet in order to obtain more data on it, it was possible to prioritize which type of investment to make (Infrastructure or security). In the first two attempts the author did not manage to complete the game successfully for not having considered the time and insurance. In its third attempt the author invested in the acquisition of insurance and became more flexible in the analysis of each comet, adjusting the level of the village depending on the likelihood of severity after some period of analysis of each comet.

If the author of this report had the opportunity to redesign the game, he would propose that as the phases were advancing new risks (related to households, crime, war, etc.) were introduced. This proposal is based on the facts that in real life are present countless risks, and this would allow the game users to understand how to manage risks in such cases.
