What was the role of risk management in the game? The role of risk management in the game was designed to protect the pods form the effects of a meteor hitting their world and causing damage. The player could choose between protective and reactive measures. Research allowed better knowledge about the risk and severity of the impact. Protection could be build in terms of upgrading the pods and raising the level of the platform. In my first try I invested most of the money into raising the platform and building a few pods. I did research on most of the meteorites and if the chances were high of an impact with great severity, I also bought the most expensive insurance. I mostly trade knowledge of for investing in protection and insurance. What did you do differently after the first time? During the first levels this strategy worked quiet well, but I lost in the fourth level due to the fact that I ran out of money to protect the pods. I my second try I build more pods from the beginning and invested more time in research and upgrading the pods. I hardly spent anything in insurance. This strategy turned out very well for the game as I made it through all levels without having casualties. Building all pods and upgrading them, brings enough money to invest in enough protection to avoid any damage from the meteorites. What change would you propose to better represent the risk management choices that matter in the place you live? The game showed that with enough knowledge and protection no insurance was necessary. Although I doubt that this situations reflects very well real world situations. Regions which are struck by natural disasters often do not have enough capital to invest into so much protection or knowledge to not need insurance at all. Development is often slow and especially in developing countries there doesn’t exist much capital to invest in expensive high tech protection measures. Also you can’t built protection against everything. I think that the game tried to explain an interesting trade of/ problem in dealing within risk prone environment, but in my opinion the risk management process was only brought down to 3 issues, whereas in reality many more factors need to be considered. I think that case studies of successful/ unsuccessful risk management would have been more useful to understand the factors involving risk management than playing the game, with this in mind that quiet a lot of people had problems with the game play as stated in the forums. I think that it would be interesting to add institutions to the game play, as in risk management local governments as well as international institutions and foreign governments play an important role due to the ongoing globalization process.
