Risk management was an important component of the game, particularly to ensure the continued development of the city, and to maintain the structural integrity of the buildings.  It was integral to take risk into account when making decisions related to the building and upgrading of new buildings particularly when taking into account cost and time constraints. 

Knowledge:  It was important to gain further knowledge regarding the risk (meteors), in order to increase the amount of time available to recover after they struck.  It was important not to neglect this aspect as it meant that sufficient research meant enough time which ensure low damage or no damage at times. 

Protection:  Protection was also important in order to reduce damage induced by the meteor strikes; however it took its toll on financial resources and reduced the energy spent on development.  At times, focusing too much on the protection side of things meant development targets where not achieved. 

Insurance: Personally I always strived to obtain the highest level of insurance, which meant that in case of damage the cost of rebuilding was far less than without it.  In the early stages of the game, it perhaps meant a decrease in financial resources towards building, however as long as targets were being met it was still a good investment.

I found that investing in the protection aspect in the earlier stages, freed up money in later levels for construction, it also meant that less time and effort was spent on protection and more on research and construction.

After playing the game a couple of times, I realised it was important not to neglect any one aspect at each level, it was important to always do research of incoming meteors, make sure the protection level was adequate, and that construction and upgrading of buildings was always ongoing.  Focusing on one more than the others would have a detrimental effect down the line.

Geographical location from the hazard would be one aspect that I would include in a new version of the game, houses closer to a hazard, in this case closer to the incoming waves, would have a greater likelihood of suffering damage in the event of a meteor strike.  This reflects hazards in real life; it could also involve aspects such as variable insurance rates depending on the property’s location.  The involvement of community within the game wasn’t clear, perhaps an important addition could also be the education of the general population, which can invariably affect how they respond and cope to hazards.
