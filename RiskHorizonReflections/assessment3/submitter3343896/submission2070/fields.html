What was the role of risk management in the game? What choices did you make and what trade-offs did you confront in terms of knowledge, protection, and insurance? In what ways did preparation for risk affect development (immediately, or after some time)?

 Basically in the simulation the island has only one source of risk: the meteorites. In real life the risks are many and from different sources. The game shows us how to make the correct combination of different economic investment, protection action, information about threads and insurance as well as community linkage.

 At the first attempt, I buy the full insurance thinking that this should be the best way to avoid severe economic impact of the hits. I was wrong! Indeed insurance helps in the recovery process after the crisis but it is more important to prevent the damage and get information about the coming meteorites.  

 Then I bought the minimal insurance, as my father taught me! I invest as much money I could in building the little house (or whatever it was), try to get as much information from the coming meteorite, raising the level of the island and make so much community connection as I could. This strategy worked fine until level 4!
