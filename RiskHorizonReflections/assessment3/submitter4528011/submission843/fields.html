The role of risk management in the game was to identify and cope with risk of natural disaster by protecting the settlement via insurance, learning about the devastating effects of the disaster and applying lessons learn to minimize damage in the future.  The lesson learned from the activity was that certain tradeoffs had to be made in order to cope with disasters.  Personally, I found that I underutilized insurance when it came to rebuilding and found that by maintaining and increasing insurance, I was able to rebuild my producing assets at a much more efficient pace that allowed for faster growth.   Preparing for the present risk was something that I found to increase over time and something that I did not grasp quickly.  Over time, I was better able to learn which tools I needed to deploy that would provide maximum protection against a disaster and provide a faster recovery time.  It got to where I was I knew exactly which levers to pull in order to get maximum benefit.  The overall experience taught me that there is not a “silver bullet” to use that will satisfactorily resolve each issue however by learning from past failures and implementing what you learned, you are able to cope.

 

I quickly found that insurance protection in this game is very important and was grossly underutilized in my early efforts.  The first few times I played this game I essentially ignored insurance and found that any impacts were devastating to my settlement and impossible to overcome.  It was only after playing the game a few times that I found the value in protecting my producing assets and I was able to recover from a disaster more quickly.  As a secondary piece, I also found the value of increasing the distance between the tide and settlement by raising the land.  The value in this to me was that I could somewhat offset the time spent learning about the asteroids which allowed for more development and more productivity.  When used in tandem, insurance and ground elevation were invaluable to me and allowed me to build my settlement much faster than I otherwise could have.  The three main things that I have learned from playing this game is the value of preparation of a risk event, that protection from uncertainty provides value to your assets and that time management is critical in a developing risk event. 

 

I currently live in a region of the country that is experiencing drought and water resources are low.  The water district that serves my community has maintained permanent water restrictions until further notice.  As water is such a critical element of life, I would suggest to my water district two proposals.  The first being to fund educational efforts to learn more about water efficiency and host classes for its citizens to understand how to use water more efficiently.  This could maybe transform itself into extending the district’s reach to water filtration systems as to capture more grey water and refine it to potable water.  Or, to seek alternative methods of water discovery.  The second proposal would be to construct additional water pipelines to areas that experience higher precipitation to be used as a plan B type scenario.  Learning from the drought is just as important as providing protection from future events in order to recover.  In the game, I would propose a plan B type scenario so that in the event that a disaster is devastating, there was a choice to flee and rebuild in another location.  In my local situation, that is exactly what I would do in the event water became scarce.
