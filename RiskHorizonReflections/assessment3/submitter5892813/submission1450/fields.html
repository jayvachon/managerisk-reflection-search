INTRODUCTION

Risk management can be a powerful instrument for development whether risks are systemic or idiosyncratic, imposed or taken on voluntarily, development can occur only by successfully confronting risk and pursuing opportunity. 

Reflection on the game:

The role of risk management in the game measures Put in place mitigation measures in the event disaster strikes, Have resources that will enable developers invest in knowledge acquisition to help us better understand potential risks, To better measure the probability of risks, To invest in protection activities to safeguard communities

Choices made and the trade-offs confronted

Knowledge: I cautiously invested in research as I thought I needed some of these resources to improve the pods as and when comets hit and caused minimal damage.Protection: Risk is inherently characterized by uncertainty. This makes it difficult to know—for certain—whether a risk management intervention will ultimately prove worthwhile. Insurance: This is critical in the event disasters hit although the costs are very high and we feel that resources paid for such covers would have been invested in developing our communities. I had to sacrifice development to have resources saved to pay for insurance to protect the few development I attempted to develop.

The effect of Risk Management/preparation on potential development;Before any developments, large shocks that are very likely to occur require adequate preparation to make it worthwhile investing in these developments. The more likely a disaster was likely to occur the more resources had to be reserved for research and building more protection for the pods. This constrained the development of more pods which would otherwise have been beneficial to the community.After developments it required even more resources for potential risks that had not been envisaged. This meant that in some cases due to inadequate preparation the Pods were exposed greatly to potential damage and in many cases the community, though with many pods they were destroyed or damaged due to inadequate preparation for potential risks.

LESSONS LEARNED FROM THE GAME

If I had to play the game more than once, I would minimize my developments and have sufficient resources to insure the few developments I have made.I would also pursue the above strategy in addition to carrying out sufficient research to ensure that I improve my knowledge on potential disasters. That way I would be prepared adequately to salvage my developments.By contrast, low-probability high-impact events required less costs for insurance.I also noticed that in some cases I spent a lot of resources in research and insurance and yet when the impacts were experienced I noted that I had probably over invested to cover my pods whose damage was minimal. I guess I was too risk averse.

Progress recognized the more I played the game:I did quite a few things differently after the first time I played the game for example I invested a lot in research to gain more knowledge in potential occurrence of comets.I also invested more in insurance to reduce the impact of comets

The main things I learned from the game:Investing in research to equips us with sufficient knowledge is costly however the cost of not investing in research on potential risks is even greater.If you spend too much on building and upgrading pods, you are exposed to comets when they hit development should not constrain research to mitigate risks.The more protection you provide your pods the less impact you are exposed to when comets hit.Insurance is a vital risk management tool. Without it developers are at high risk of losing the entire investment

PROPOSED ADDITIONS TO BETTER REPRESENT THE RISK MANAGEMENT CHOICES THAT MATTER:I would add in varying risks with differing magnitudes so that players are able to choose which risk to prioritize.I would also create a scenario where players have to have some reserve resources for contingencies to enable them to confront new or disasters that are not common in the game or a given area.
