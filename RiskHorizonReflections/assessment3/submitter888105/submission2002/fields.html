The game reflects the following points among others:


• Riskhorizon is pascal tool of assessing the effectiveness of Research, Protection and Insurance measures in buliding resilience in face of disaster imminence. There may therefore be scope for developing core indicators of performance and a standard typology of disasters to enable a better assessment of both baseline trends and the performance of research measurement.


• The imminence of disasters caused by sudden extreme climatic events (meteors) – a wonder of that game - impact climate change on disaster resilience therefore needs to be considered. The risk assessment and disaster resilience strategy also need to take into account broader issues that will have implications on vulnerability and disaster risk. This could include population increase, migration and price rises much more that single indicator of pods


• Riskhorizon game is a good tool that employs 'Use values' that estimate values for the goods and services lost due to a disaster event. If these are items, then are trade off  in the place, then cost can be used as a proxy of the value of environmental goods and services. For services e.g recreational value derived by the natural environment, and non-use values e.g value of knowing that a certain species will not become extinct, these can be estimated through a more survey-based approach such as contingent valuation method


• At a single play of the game, more energy concentrated on building a capacity insurance at the expense of limited resource(cost) is later believed to have concentrated on pods farer from clif edge to reinforce protection at lower cost referenced to time constrain.


• More information on the extent to which Research, Protection and Insurance measures reduce the impact of disasters. This could take the form of a “ready reckoner”, which would take account of factors such as nature of hazard, the number of persons affected, local geography etc to help identify or

prioritise appropriate types of Research, Protection and Insurance measures.


• Similarly, a manual for simplified project appraisal at Research, Protection and Insurance project level

may be useful to help those implementing Research, Protection and Insurance measures to select the best options, and to help programme sponsors evaluate expenditure


• An analysis of which Research, Protection and Insurance measures also yield significant non-Research, Protecchon and insurance development benefits (or costs), and the extent to which they do so, could

potentially help mainstream Research, Protection and Insurance into other development programmes.


• More guidance or tools on disaster proofing critical social and economic infrastructure (such as hospitals, emergency services, key transport routes and telecommunications) would be useful if I was to add more. Methods of disaster proofing, and assessment of the costs and benefits of protecting critical infrastructures could be additional tools within the Research, Protection and Insurance of the community or habitat.
