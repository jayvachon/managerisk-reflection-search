As an anti – computer game player, I must say that I found this game
particularly funny and entertaining (even though it got blocked a
couple of times :)). What I liked most about it is that it made me
think continuously and forced me into finding equilibrated strategies
and solutions for limit situations, when more options are available
and resources are limited (both time and money). And this is mostly
what risk management is about.
In terms of preparation, I mainly concentrated on knowledge (research)
and one protection measure (raising the isle) and this was generally
the most important trade-off I did during the game, every time I
played. Nevertheless, while sometimes I neglected constant development
concentrating more on insurance to diminish the loss, I found that
upgrading the pods seemed to make them more resistant and increase the
overall financial resources. Thus, sometimes I chose to diminish the
investment in insurance and use the available resources for the
development of the world.
In the short run, it was obvious that raising the island (protection)
would diminish the power of each impact. Then, continuous investment
in research helped a lot in loss reduction (through community help –
if you are trained to do the connections fast enough:)) after each
impact. I noticed that the better one manages to prepare for the risk
(through knowledge, protection and insurance) and cope with it
(community help after the hits), the smaller losses were and the more
financial resources one saved to develop the managed world (build and
upgrade pods).
I played the game three times. The best option at the end proved to be
constantly investing in the research, then balance the remaining
resources between insurance (I decided to choose for the middle
option, after many variations), the constant development of the pods
(which increased the total financial resources – I had neglected this
part at the beginning) and raising the isle to diminish the power of
the comet hits.
What I found particularly interesting at the game was the move from a
non existent to a developed world (going through the development
phase, as well). Investment in the pods (through construction and
upgrading) increased the financial resources and, at the same time,
the costs of other services (such as insurance, which was directly
related to the values we had to insure). Nevertheless, it also proved
to be more resistant to shocks, as well. What I would add is the
communication of knowledge (many times, knowledge is not disseminated
correctly or through the correct and trustworthy resources). Research
is not sufficient if people do not know about its results and impacts.
