1.         Risk management played a big role in the game: the elements of knowledge, protection and insurance were all crucial. When I played the game, I chose to do research to gain knowledge about each comet on the horizon: if the ‘chance of hit’ and ‘severity’ were both low, I would disregard the comet, and I would not use resources to buy insurance or increase protection (raising the island). However, if the ‘chance of hit’ and ‘severity’ were both high, I would buy insurance that would provide 75% coverage, in order to minimize losses after a probable hit. I would also spend time and money on protection to reduce the level of damage to the pods. I confronted some trade-offs: if I spent more time on research to get more accurate knowledge about a comet, I did not have as much time to use resources to raise the island for protection, or to build/upgrade pods for development. Likewise, due to limited resources, I sometimes had to decide whether to build/upgrade pods, raise the island, or buy insurance, as I did not have enough resources to do everything. Risk preparation slightly hindered development in the short term because I had less resources for development, but preparation aided development in the longer term, as I was better protected and/or insured against damages that would significantly hinder development.

2.         The first time I played the game, I forgot to protect the island or buy insurance because I was too focused on development (building/upgrading pods) that I neglected risk management. As a result, the damage caused by a comet was so great that I lost on that level. The next time I played, I made sure to buy insurance on each level if I believed that there was a high risk of a severe comet hitting. After playing a few times, I discovered that protection (raising the island) was a better game strategy than buying insurance, because undamaged pods contribute more to development than damaged pods. The main things I learned from the game is that risk management contains different elements (learning about a risk, protecting against the risk, and insuring the risk), and I learned that risk management is a balancing act, as a person’s resources are limited. I also learned that protecting against a risk is better than only buying insurance for potential losses.

3.         A change I would propose is to add more risks to the game. There are the risks of flooding, contagious diseases, and financial crises in the place I live. There are several risk management choices that matter here, such as deciding where to live (living on a hill means that flooding is less likely but it's more expensive).
