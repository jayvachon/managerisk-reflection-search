The role of risk management in the game was to teach the players to be well prepared for risks so that minimum damage is caused when a disaster strikes. While playing the game I always made sure that I had at least 50% insurance so that if a meteorite struck the island, it would cause minimum damage. Also I made sure that I had protection at least equivalent to the severity of the meteorite. Preparation for risk according to me affected development immediately, as development can be seen in the level of preparedness for the risk.

 

When I played the game for the second time I made sure I did more research to measure the severity of the meteorite hit which I did not do when I played for the first time. While playing this game I learnt that insurance is a very important component of risk management and the cost of insurance pays off when a disaster strikes. It helps one to cope with the damages caused by the disaster. Also researching about the disasters and the different kinds of risks, helps one to be better prepared and reduces the impact of the damages.

 

If I had the option to add something to the game I would provide a variety of risks (like floods, earthquakes etc.), so that the player understands that there are different kinds of risks one is prone to and one should be prepared for all those risks. Also it would teach the player that different risks need to be tackled in different ways. In real life my surroundings are prone to earthquakes and also flooding due to the sea being close by. People living here and in other areas should be well prepared for any kind of risk that the area has faced in the past and also any other future potential risks.
