 In my opinion it gave us a somewhat reality check on how to deal with uncertainties in this case the meteors while achieving the objective of growing pods in  a limited time span. In order to meet the objective and counter the risk three tools in the form of research, protection and insurance were available. So when playing the game I opted for various combinations first was dominated by protection and insurance but it was not after long that I discovered that protection was too slow and did not match the speed with which the meteors hit. Then I traded protection with knowledge and 75% insurance coverage. This combination worked really well for me, occasionally I relied on protection but the most used tools in my case were research and insurance. Initially in my case the preparation for risk management was more of a trial and error however after multiple re-tries it ensured development.

If I played the game only once,  I would have started by getting some knowledge but not too much (not constantly doing research till the meteor is reached the water) followed by protection and then lastly insurance whereby the percentage of insurance coverage depending on the available resources but my aim would be to get 75% coverage. If I played the game more than once which I did, I learned that doing research should not be stopped till the time you are confident that the meteor would not cause any damage and continue getting as much information about the severity and chances of hit as possible. Then after evaluating the severity and the chances of hit I would opt for insurance coverage followed by protection as in my experience it was not of much use. There were quite a few things that I learned from the game first of all the timing of using the tools, as each tool can be used at a particular time like in the case of protection unless you started it earlier as a precautionary tool it would not give to enough time to raise the level and protect against the hit once the meteor appears. Similarly gaining knowledge is an ongoing process and it is important to keep tabs on it throughout the risk cycle as new research might suggest a change in the level of severity or chances of hit and eventually guide you to make a smart move. Another important lesson was that insurance coverage is vital as an individual would find it extremely difficult to bear the entire loss if some of the risk is covered it gives a bit of confidence to plant more pods/ take risky options without the fear of losing it all.

If I could change one thing I would give the player an option to buy a meteor shooting machine which would cost around $600 and can only be purchased once to shoot up to 5 meteors.
