RISK AND OPPORTUNITY: MANAGING RISK FOR DEVELOPMENT- REFLECTION

To manage risk efficiently one must have information, resources and insurance in order to protect oneself from uncalled for events. The role of risk management in the game was to be always prepared because the comet could strike Alora at any time. The choices that I made was to always have insurance at all times. I did this because I noticed that rebuilding was cheaper with insurance than without it and therefore I always made sure I has at least the least costing Insurance if the not the costlier one. Given the fact that knowledge, protection and insurance are essential, these three factors do not work independently. They are interdependent and this makes risk management efficient. Just having knowledge would not suffice as you cannot battle a crisis in the best possible manner without the rest and the same scenario would apply to protection and insurance. In the game, preparing for the risk affects the development immediately. A good chunk of financial resources would go away due to the investment in insurance and elevating the area of Alora to protect it from the waves and comets. After a hit from comet the developments would again slow down as time and resources are again spent on repairing the damaged caused.

When I played the game for the first time I concentrated too much only on development, insurance and protection. And if I had played the game only once I should have also spent more time on research. On the other hand I played the game more than once, therefore I did try my best to strike a balance on Development, Protection, Insurance and Research. The take away the I got from playing this game was to be geared up to address whatever situation that pose a threat to survival as the world is full of uncertainties.

Agriculture plays vital role in many countries and India is one such country. As I live in India agriculture here is said to have accounted for 17% of its GDP in the 2012. Therefore instead of comets I would propose to base risk on Rainfall. About 60-70% of India’s water is utilised for the purpose of agriculture and without rainfall the country would go haywire. This change would be substantial here because I learnt in week one about index insurance wherein the index would be based on rainfall and thus such an insurance would benefit exceptionally in managing risk.

So, to conclude by playing the game one learns how development, information, protections, insurance, resources, research etc works together and one cannot really judge which aspect is more important than the other. Hence it essential to look at all the aspects simultaneously to win over a disaster.
