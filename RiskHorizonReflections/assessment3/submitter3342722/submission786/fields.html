Risk and Opportunity: Managing Risk for Development


Reflective Assignment


I played the game several times to get a feeling of the different dynamics and strategies one can pursue. Generally, I found that there are some interesting trade-offs at play in the game. The most significant being between research and protection because they both take time whereas insurance can be bought on very short notice without any downside. But I felt that the most pressing trade-off was risk management in general and development in general, not so much different parts within risk management. So it was quite interesting how to deal with shocks once they had occured (which led to failure almost all of the time) and how to prioritize development and risk prevention. I found that preparation for risk and development could be done simultaneously to some degree. You always had to have some capital for last-minute insurance as well as keep in mind that increasing protection cost resources and slowed development. Apart from that, upgrading pods and risk prevention was in no way mutually exclusive since one required just a single click.

After playing once or twice with few buildings and upgrading these continuously I began to feel that this was not the best strategy. In my third attempt I used all the plots to build pods first and upgraded them together which turned out to be way more cost-effective. But this only concerned development. When it comes to risk management I definitely learned that prevention is preferrable because once a shock hit you, you were very unlikely to recover in time.

To improve the game, one might consider implementing certain features which affect the likelihood of a shock to occur. This would capture the fact that effective risk management can also mean reducing the vulnerability of a certain population. Also, it might be interesting to have different plots where pods can be placed. So that the plots closer to the sea would suffer more severe damages in case of a shock whereas those further away would be more secure. To make it more interesting one could make the more vulnerable pods more profitable in terms of development allowing for a riskier game style.
