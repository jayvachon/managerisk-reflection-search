In the game protection helped most, then insurance and knowledge helped in preparation. All three components were of great use but I feel measures taken to mitigate risk were most effective and so knowledge. More advanced pods you have less the damage and less efforts to re-establish them. Spending on research and having some information upfront helped in making decisions regarding the allocation of resources between insurance and protection. Also, it helped in addressing specific pod to lower damage. Preparedness and Protection measures were best ways to mitigate risk. So, I focused more on constructing pods and maintaining them and stuck with 50% insurance. I used knowledge to understand severity and chances of HITS. Preparation helped both immediately and after the hit. Well prepared pods reduced the probability and size of losses and they also the maintenance costs were pretty less after hits.

 

I f I will play again, I will focus more on understanding risk and based on that I will upgrade my pods.I guess, risk cannot be mitigated by general precautions (making all pods and upgrading them because you have limited funds). One should apply knowledge to understand shocks and its intensity and based on that use potent measures to recover from losses and make the most benefits out of those protection measures. Insurance too help in coping after risk. It requires strategic approach where you have to logically understand the possible risk and allocate your resources in promising manner.

 

 

I guess game depicts major aspects of risk management considering the fact that it delivers message of risk management in a very simplified and effective way. In the game, I will add option of planting trees and digging ponds. Trees will certainly help in absorbing shock and ponds will help in managing water overflow from the sea due to hits. In my area, I will promote people to people connect. In addition to Knowledge, Protection, Insurance and Coping, what can significantly help in mitigating risk is interpersonal relation among people of a community i.e. community feeling. Connected people can reach to a consensus about preparedness and implementation of pre risk activities smoothly and efficiently. As highlighted in the course, one of the biggest obstacle is willingness to pay attention beforehand i.e. procrastination. With community feeling, well informed people can not only collaborate and minimize the risk but can help each other; coping the risk with sense of togetherness. Risk management can be effective with participation whether it is in preparedness or coping.
