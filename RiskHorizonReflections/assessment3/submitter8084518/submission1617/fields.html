The primary threat to Alora, the area we are protecting in this game, is comet attack. Comet attacks would introduce a number of shocks, such as flooding, financial collapse, unemployment and so forth.

Risk management under this circumstances is to grow a community while preparing it for comet hits and coping with the damages and threats ensuing by comets attacks.

Attempting to achieve this goal, my strategy is, firstly, to construct two or three pods, and then upgrade them to a certain level, therefore to form a relatively strong community connection as a kind of insurance, so that the pods can be less damaged during disasters (comet hits). On the other hand, I also insured them according to the severity and chance of hit of the upcoming comets.

However, this strategy didn’t serve well. To begin with, information of the comets are changeable. Sometimes it will cause severe damage to Alora even the shown chance of hit is zero. On top of that, the effectiveness of protection is hard to predict. Upgrading or connecting the pods could raise protection level, as instructed by this game, however, there is no clear index or data could show the protection taken by the decision maker is strong enough or not. Moreover, buying insurance depending on the information (severity, for instance) of the comets will sometimes make it too late to get Alora insured if the shock is imminent.  Furthermore, be it monitoring the comets, upgrading or connecting pods, buying insurance or repairing damaged pods, take times for them to work. Last but not least, resources such as money, attention and time are limited. These conflicts were getting more and more intensive when shocks reoccur more frequently. Struggling to balancing these trade-offs, I kept failing this game at level 2.

Therefore, I played this game few more times and altering the strategy. Gradually, I found that to prevent Alora from hitting by comets is not the mission of risk management in this case, instead, lowering the damage level is. Therefore, I lift the height of Alora according to the severity and chance of hit of the comets in order to reduce the impact of flood and waves. It turned out that proper protected pods are suffered less or even no damage. Moreover, insured pods are cheaper to repair and this would help to manage the conflict between constructing and upgrading pods and repairing fees. Thus, I insured Alora at the beginning of every level of the game.

This game is a very good trial for us to check our understanding of risk management as well as our ability to manage risk in a given situation. However, I found this game would be more representative, if no less interesting, if it could, firstly, have more tools for us to choose to manage shocks. Building seawall could be a good way to prevent flood and waves that caused by comet hitting other than just lifting the ground; secondly, introduce more social actors; and thirdly, introduce internal conditions such as population stress and some other kinds of risks, just to name some.
