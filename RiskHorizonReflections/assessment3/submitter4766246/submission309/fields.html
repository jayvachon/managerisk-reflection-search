The Game was interesting from the point of view of risk management.  The Game exemplifies that the risk management can be a powerful instrument for development.   Risk management can save lives and mitigate the impact of economic and other shocks.  Risk management practices involves acquiring knowledge (research), protection (insurance) and coping which involves updating knowledge, learning from the experience, rebuilding infrastructure and lives and community networks.  The choices used in the game include balancing development with risk management: financial resources have to be apportioned to development and risk mitigating measures (such as insurance and other safeguard activities).  These are the choices and trade off.  As one prepares for risk, financial resources are utilized and development activities are put on hold. A key take-away is that risk management is not a one-off event.  It is a process which must be understood by individuals and the community at large; systematic operations should be embedded throughout the risk chain.

The game was played several times.  The key is using the learning in previous games to inform decision making – such as buying insurance and building defenses early and taking a phased and planned approach to development.  The man things I learnt from the game are:

1.       The main types of risk management tools are knowledge, protection and coping.

2.       Effective community networks could reduce impact of the shocks

3.       Risk management actions (knowledge and protection) should be taken in advance.

4.       impact of shocks could be amplified or reduce depending on your environment and risk measures

5.       Past shocks can affect the severity of future shocks – be prepared

6.       Effective risk management involves combining capacity to prepare and cope, such as building strong community networks pool resources and share risks.

7.       Risk management costs money, but the benefits clearly outweigh the costs

8.       Early warning system could be an effective risk management tool

9.       Uncertainty is an obstacle that could impair risk management

What was snot evident in the game is that it is essential to have a strong emergency response system in place, to ensure that it works, and to test it. This system should be designed for worst-case scenarios.

The change I would recommend to the game has to do with the mechanics.  Failing Levels higher than one, forces the player to restart the game.  I would recommend that the player should have to choice of restarting or continuing from the previous level.  In terms of content, it would be great if behavioural obstacles could be added, namely, over-confidence, under-estimation of the risk and procrastination.  In my part of the world, these behavioural matters are most problematic from the point of view of risk management. Also, the role of public institutions in coping with shocks could be added - public hospitals and clinics, public assistance, and the work of civil defense bodies.
