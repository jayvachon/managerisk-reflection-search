My reflections on the Game:

While the game was great to test my reflexes for a computer game- highlighting the concept of risk and opportunity, it failed to teach me much about any strategy for building a resilient community through protection, knowledge and insurance totally leaving out the social cultural aspects. It is simply a Computer Game and like any other Computer game, the more I played the better I got at reaching the higher levels. As I am not a computer game player, it took me some time to get the hang of it which as a working professional was valuable time lost.

 Even then, I liked the concept of the game and played it many time, some for just trying to reach the higher level and at other times to test an idea of doing it differently.

Risk Management was all about identification, assessing and prioritising of risk to the development activities and well being of the people and taking appropriate steps through instruments like protection and insurance. As per my understanding from the game the choices for development –planning, budget and timeline depended on funds availability. While there is unpredictability of the natural hazard like comet falling and neither the frequency nor the time of occurrence of comet is known  it is important that our knowledge  be enhanced for better preparedness and mitigation through monitoring, analysing and then taking action. Insurance is available to offer protection from the risks, but insurance choices are difficult as it involves economic trade-off’s and hence the % of insurance had to be carefully thought, keeping in mind the development as well as protection needs. Lack of knowledge about the comets frequency and severity of occurrences lead to over or under insurance and similar was in the case with development activities resulting is losses.The game tried to teach us how to maintain a balance between knowledge, protection and insurance to adopt risk sensitive development practices.

If the idea was to teach risk management, I feel that the game will have to be reworked a bit to include parameters which highlights the reasons for our wrong decisions. Further, loss or gain estimation for each comet hit or missed comet strike should be added to provide us with a better understanding of importance of risk management. The game is built on three principles protection, insurance and knowledge. The tutorials need to be improved as did not provide clear instructions and was not very clear about the protection wall and the community linking through lighted circles/balls.

 Abha Mishra
