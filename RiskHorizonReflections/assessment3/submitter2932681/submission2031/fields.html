The role of risk management in the game was to evaluate the risk that each of the meteors brought to the village and prepare in the best way possible for the consequences.

Regarding the trade-offs, the important thing in my opinion was to evaluate the risk posed by the meteors and the capital you had invested in the city. In the initial phases, I chose not to insure the buildings and try to build enough protection to deal with the threats posed by the meteors. Even though sometimes it was not enough, the cost of repairs was small and not worth of insurance. As the building became more advance it forced me to take a more conservative approach purchasing more insurance since the damage costs were much larger and there was many more meteors. Knowledge was used mainly in meteors who posed bigger treats, with significant probabilities of impact and damage, it was a cheap tool but a bit time consuming.

 It was clear that using the resources according to the risk permitted a better allocation of resources indicating moments where you should concentrate on dealing with the risk and preparing for immediate treats and moments where risk was low and effort was concentrated on development of the village.

To be honest it took me a while to understand completely all the aspects from the game, including its objective. The introduction misses to explain some of the key features of the game. In the beginning I was very conservative and try to prepare for the meteors, trying to reduce at all cost its impacts, without actually putting much though in the trade-offs. I invested heavily on protection and insurance and did not pay much attention to knowledge. As I kept failing on the 3rd phase I started to pay more attention on how to balance my investment and choose the best time to invest in each of the resources available.

The thing that I would change in the game is the way that the resources are used. As I understood, in the game we were supposed to be responsible for COORDINATING the actions and not EXECUTING them. Therefore, for that matter, resources should not be time consuming for the player, like in building protection where you have to keep pressing on the button for more protection, and knowledge where you have to keep following the meteor for it to grow. You should be able to order something and things get done, without direct supervision. Once you chose to invest knowledge on a meteor it should keep monitoring until it passes the planet and have a fixed cost per period of monitoring time. I think that would be a big improvement in the game since it would capture reality best and leave the player with more time to calculate the trade-offs.
