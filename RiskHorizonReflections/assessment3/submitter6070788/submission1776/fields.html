As the Luminator – the policy-maker of Alora – I was responsible for making decisions concerning the prosperity and well-being of the population. With the constraint of limited financial resources, my goal was to promote economic and social development but also to watch out for shocks and promptly face them. Alora in fact is subject to falling comets, which careening into the sea cause devastating waves. Shocks, i.e. comet hits, in the game correspond to real-life natural disasters, such as floods, earthquakes, tsunami.

In my gaming experience, it all came down to finding the right balance between promoting community development (by upgrading the pods) and pursuing preparation for risk (by investing in the three components of risk management: research, protection and insurance). Research consists in gaining crucial knowledge about comets: their severity and their chance of hit. Protection entails raising the island with respect to the sea level, so that the island can withstand the crashing waves. Protection can also be boosted by connecting the yellow glows, an in-game action which represent the act of creating community connections. Insurance requires to choose from a set of insurance plans: after a shock, pods can be rebuilt at a cheaper price and in less time, depending on the plan selected.

Of course, I found myself confronting significant trade-offs on multiple occasions: limited financial resources meant I had to allocate money in a certain direction and then wait before I could perform another investment, possibly compromising the well-being of my people. However, during the game I personally experienced how preparation for risk strongly affected development: when protection against comets was high enough, the pods received no damage. Having survived the comet strike unscathed, I did not have to spend money in repairing the pods and I could therefore immediately boost development by upgrading them. Risk management indeed allows to reduce the damages and to benefit sooner from the opportunities of development.

I played the game multiple times, advancing progressively to higher levels. With respect to the first time I played, in later attempts I invested more and sooner in protection and I extensively researched comets only when their severity and/or chance of hit were higher.

If I had to suggest an improvement to the game, I would propose to include information dissemination towards the population of Alora, i.e. the dwellers in the pods. This could be implemented in different ways, for example by investing in the organization of emergency drills and evacuation simulations, or by investing in the purchase of warning systems, such as alarms, loudspeakers and so on. A more involved, informed and prepared population is a more resilient population and it would possibly result in less casualties and damages.
