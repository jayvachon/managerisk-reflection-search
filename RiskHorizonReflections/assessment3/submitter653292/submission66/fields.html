The concept of the game was closely related to the issues of risk management covered within the video lecture and required reading; the purpose of the game was to develop a community whilst balancing a trade-off between knowledge (researching the meteorite properties), protection (building defences which halted growth), and insurance (different levels could be purchased).  Initially, the majority of meteorites had low impact and so the community could withstand this without much protection, however as the game progressed the protection had to be increased – this had to be done gradually otherwise there would be insufficient development to complete the level.

After getting wiped out by the meteorite at the end of the 2nd level, I played the game several times; each time I placed more effort in building protection at earlier stages of the game. I also found that engaging in more “even” development, rather than building a few properties to high-levels, was helpful in generating growth – perhaps this was an example of spreading risk improving the opportunities afterwards. I picked up three main lessons; firstly, early preparation of protection can be helpful in the future (even if a natural disaster does not hit in the initial period). Secondly, research into / gaining knowledge of the threat can be helpful in deciding what measures to take (for instance if it was clear that a meteorite was going to be low impact and have a low probability of hitting then one could concentrate on other items of development). Lastly, a good reaction to the event (i.e. clicking on the developments after a meteorite hits) can minimise the damage incurred.

The one change I would make is allowing a differential impact on the properties depending on where they are positioned on the map relative to the impact; for instance those positioned nearest to the impact would have greater damage. This would encourage users to build away from the worst hit areas and is akin to the information in the videos that suggest flood-damage and earthquake damage in the developing world can be reduced by building in different areas.
