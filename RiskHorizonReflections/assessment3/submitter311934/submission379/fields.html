The game Risk Horizon taught the importance of risk management in developing a community. The growth of a community should not just focus on developing the community but also on managing the risks. This is because without the risk management, the potential impact of the risk can be detrimental to the growth of the community.

With the game, balancing the development (developing the pods) and risk management (research and insurance) is the tricky part. Yes, in order to progress in the game, the development quota should be achieved in each level, but the impact of the comet, if not addressed, can easily reverse its growth. To achieve the game’s objective, I made the following choices/tradeoffs/decisions:

1.       Purchased the cheapest insurance for each level.

2.       I did not just stop in achieving the expected development for each level but ensured that I am ahead in developing my pods to prepare for the next levels.

3.       I did not waste my time doing research on low-severity-and-low-chance-of-hitting comets.

4.       If the comet is a high-severity-and-high-chance-of-hitting, I do ample research on them to increase my knowledge and community connection.

5.       During idle times, I ensure that I increase my level of protection.

All these activities did not happen one at a time but some happened simultaneously. For example, while upgrading my pods, I do research on high-severity-and-high-chance-of-hitting or I increase my protection.

This strategy I have come up with did not come after playing the game once but after several attempts of playing. In the first few attempts I focused too much on managing the risk and too little on development thus I was unable to reach level 6. Then in my next attempts, I tried to balance risk management and development but still failed because I only on short term goal and that is to pass the particular level. But this game taught me to think strategically in terms of my decisions if I want to win. Hence, as much as possible I continued to upgrade my pods even if the development meter is colored green already to ensure that I have enough income for the succeeding levels.  

Moreover, this game taught me that in order to achieve growth, several risks can be neglected – the risks with low severity and probability. And to learn if the risk is with low severity and probability, it is important to study and do research on the possible risks to occur. Being proactive and not reactive when it comes to risks is critical to development. This can help in planning and in strategizing the decisions to be made.

Lastly, risk management does entail expenses (money and time) and can immediately affect the development process but the long term benefits of risk management in development are worth the price. There are uncertainties that can be avoided if a good management plan was made. For example, building evacuation centers for flood-prone areas is a good solution to avoid deaths during a calamity.  Another example is by purchasing insurance, though it can be expensive, will surely minimize the potential shock that can be experienced during a disaster.

 Now, if given the chance to change some components of the game, I will suggest that there are technologies that a player has the option to invest at in order to further minimize the impact of risks. This can further test the player’s decision making in putting a balance between risk management and development. Also, this decision to invest or not is a good test to us coming from third world countries where money is important and every cent matters.
