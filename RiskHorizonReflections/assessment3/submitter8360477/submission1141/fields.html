The first time that I played the game, I attempted to build several pods and upgrade as quickly as possible, but my research quotient was not very high and my insurance rate was only at 50% of potential loss. When a meteor hit, I did not have sufficient time to repair my pods before the time had expired.  I worked very quickly to upgrade the pods as I could, but I needed to balance that with insurance, repair, and research costs. My inclination was to turn to insurance as a way to mitigate risk, rather than relying on research capacity. I attempted the game several more times, but was unable to find the appropriate balance between research, insurance, and upgrading to beat the level. 

If I were to play again, I would invest in fewer pods, but ensure that each pod was built to its maximum upgrade availability. My expectation is that investing in this upgrade will allow superior performance from the pods and minimal damage from advancing and damaging meteors. The research quotient did not seem to add much to my score and would not be a priority for me.

If I could change one aspect of the game, I would add the option to borrow funds from other locales with a relatively high interest rate. This would evidence the reality of needing to borrow from surrounding and/or altruistic countries and may need to be a last-resort, but realistic option in the game.
