The role of risk management determines the success of medium and long term development goal.  However, the effective risk management coping strategy relies very much on the combination of the knowledge on risk, protection from risk and transferring risk (insurance). In game, there is challenge for decision maker to invest in research and protection because s/he doesn’t know how much the impact would be reduced.  Another trade-off between the research and protection is related to the cost and time availability. Although research doesn’t cost anything (in game), it requires a lot of time, which is the precious resource. Increase the protection costs so much, but at the same time, it stalls the development. Thus, this trade-off is for decision maker to balance on the investment on research and protection. Unlike understanding on risk and community protection, insurance scheme could cover certain percentage of damage. The preparation of risk management could protect community from devastation, but affects the resource allocation to the development: The more investment in risk management, the less resources (time and capital) available for community development, which jeopardizes the development goal.

I played game multiple times. At the beginning, I chose development growth path with premium insurance scheme, without realizing the importance of research. This strategy allowed me to win level two or three only. When the severe impact or due catastrophes hit in the level four, the community failed backward very far and the development goal became unreachable. Later on I saw the importance of research on reducing the damage, but I couldn’t reach the level four. Finally, I changed the strategy by focusing on research on risk and pushing development in correlation with level of protection with using fund from not buying insurance in level one and two. However, I started level three with buying premium insurance then focusing on protection and research. The development growth was invested moderately, while the protection level was kept high above average. Though it was costly policy, the damage was relatively small, that I could win the level three. In level four, I kept using this strategy with slightly increasing the level of protection, and I won level four. In level five, the severity of damage became higher, so did the demand for development growth rate. This was so challenging because increasing the protection and research consumes the fund and time for development. In addition, the cost of development and insurance was higher than previous stages of development. With high severity comet strike, the development goal became so far. Although there was fund for re-habitation, the development rate was too slow. Ultimately, I ended game in level five.  

To improve the game, there are some changes. By using more realistic things instead of pods construction and comet strike, e.g. buildings construction in a community confronting risk of typhoons. The game lacks one important aspect of risk management, which is soft measure and hard measure of protection.
