The game was the exact replica of the title of this MOOC – ‘Risk and Opportunity: Managing Risk for Development’, where ‘risk’ was those falling comets whereas money, insurance and protection constituted the term ‘opportunity’. Apart from these constituents, ‘time’ also played a crucial role because it’s also necessary to take appropriate action at the right time because all the actions in the risk management go in vain if they are not initiated at the time required (and time management was necessary too to win the game actually).  Honestly speaking, I was unable to clear the game at the first attempt and I took four.

Risk management plays a very crucial role in the path of development and no development process is complete without confronting risk. Risk can never be reduced but it is actually transferred, for example, risk of loss due to natural calamity can be transferred to insurance company by taking an insurance policy but it cannot be reduced.

The game pushes to think strategically and pick right choices at the right time from the limited options available.  It was necessary to keep co-ordination among research, protection and insurance so that development process can move on its regular pace. Knowledge was the base to take any of the decisions regarding insurance and protection. When the severity was high of the comet, I went for high protection and when severity was not so high, I focused on taking development initiatives. I always preferred buying the best insurance plans so that maximum compensation can be recovered against the losses.

I played the game more than once. During the first attempt, I relied only on insurance cover but I learnt that being proactive is far more important than purchasing cover because insurance compensates for the losses while protective measures help in reducing the damages. One lesson I felt very interesting is that adopting protective measures never go in vain; they will always pay you back when needed.

The tool I would like to add in this game is for better decision making capability. Some research work should be done on how many people actually want to participate in the protective measures so that government can consider other alternatives and it’s very essential to get feedback of all the measures previously taken.

In the end, all I would like to say is that it was such a great learning experience and it will definitely help me applying these principles in the real world.
