Having to protect the community of ‘Alora’ from falling comets gave me the chance not only to avoid large risks on people’s lives but also gave me the opportunity to prepare and cope with that risks in order to grow and develop a healthy community. This is where risk management can be the tool for development by taking the appropriate choices.

Having played the game more than once, I started realizing that mostly boosting protection was not the solution to overcome the floods. So I began with gaining knowledge and measuring the risks from comets in terms of probability and impact. That was the key because it made me more risk taker by matching the level of protection to the minimum giving me the time to develop my community bonds because connected neighbors helped me when it was needed. When protection itself was not enough, insurance helped me when it needed. The trade-off here was that insurance out weighted the costs by repairing them quicker, at the minimum and replacing lost development.

So having gained after some time a balance between knowledge, protection and insurance, it unleashed the opportunity to grow my community and allocate more resources for development as I was going to a higher level and be more confident to cope with future shocks.  The main things that I learned from the game were that even in a game where simplified rules of risk management are applied, even that kind of large systemic risks as falling comets can be overcome and prosperity can be gained. The risk of inaction and failure would not let do anything. Moreover by talking calculated risks, reduction measurements and sharing resources with insurance and community made me thinking that preparation and coping with risk cannot be distinguished and are key interrelated components of a good risk management framework.

If I was tasked to play that game depended on my internal and external conditions I would add risk transfer mechanisms with the help of the international community since the current systemic risks of recent financial crisis in my country arise mainly from government failures, corruption and distortionary policies. So pooling resources, expertise and coordination would be a grateful help in order for both systems helping each other and overcome the problems.
