I loved playing the Risk Horizon game. I believe it helped me a lot to learn by heart the core concepts of risk management we studied so far.

 The role of risk management in the game was to develop the community of Alora. As the community grew, it’s economy has developed and there was more money to spend on new pods and in ways to protect them. I had to choose between the construction of new pods, buying insurance, investing in protection and develop research to protect Alora from the comets. As the community grew, I had to invest more in preparation for risks so the community kept growing and I didn’t need to rebuild the pods.

The game seemed to be complex at first, but it concepts correlates a lot with what we learned from the first two weeks of the course. So as I played the game, it made a lot of sense to me why should I do research to avoid disasters from the comets (you have to do research to prevent and prepare to great disasters), why should I connect between the pods when a comet hits (the people in the community has to help each other to cope with a disaster after it happens) and why more pods would create more money (that would be a metaphor to economies of scale, and also means that a more developed country has more money to invest in risk management and it’s worthy to do it to keep growing).

 I wouldn’t change a lot in the game, but it came to my attention that it’s possible to win the game without buying insurance – that’s what I did, because it made my life simpler.
