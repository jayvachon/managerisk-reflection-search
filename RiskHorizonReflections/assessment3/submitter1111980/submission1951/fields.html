I must admit the game was quite a struggle for me. I am not sure if there was some technical problem on my side, but I was not able to visibly profit from the community connection minigame. I was clicking and connecting the highlighted buildings, but there was no indication that I would be doing it right and even though I tried to click on them and connect them, I was still receiving very high damage. Similarly, I was frustrated from getting protection. I do not know whether it was intentional, but it was charging incredibly slowly and no matter how long I was holding the button up, I was not able to charge it more than to the first bar. Like this, even after over 7 attempts, I was not able to make it over the second round, where I was struggling with excessive damages and lack of wealth.

Let’s describe now shortly how I have approached the game. Of course, the basis was to build the buildings and get some insurance in order to make reparation cheaper. The higher the insurance the better, however in the second round, there was less resources available so in most cases, I ended up with 50% insurance. I tried to build some protection, which seemed important at the beginning, but as I was not able to charge the bar significantly, it played a less significant role in the later stages of the game for me.  When the meteorites started approaching, I tried to research each of them at least to a certain manner. The higher the chance to hit and the higher its destructive power, the more I tried to research them. Each of them thus got at least a bit of attention, as first it seems the community help might be important (even thought later it proved to be practically useless). Only in the case of very low probability of a hit, I rather invested the time to building protection and repairing the damaged buildings from previous hits.

All in all, it is difficult to make any conclusions from the game, as I am not sure if I got it right/ it was working right. However, I learned that in order to win, one had to combine all the tools he had and consider the probability of the risk. When there were no resources for protection or costly new constructions, it was better to invest your time in free research leading to (in this case useless) community help = invest in the least costly and most easily obtainable tool. Protection might have helped, but it was not efficient, as it took a lot of time to obtain it and the results were disappointing. Insurance was for sure useful, however, in some situations, it was more useful to obtain a cheaper one rather than wait for sufficient resources.
