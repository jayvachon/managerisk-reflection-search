Risk management which is based on three key components: Preparation (knowledge), protection and insurance, represents a good combination to face risks hitting communities.

My task was to boost the ability of the world of Alora to resist to the waves evolved by the falling of comets.

In fact, in the beginning of the game, I had to grasp how to balance between my tools and how to use them properly in order to diminish the gravity of the damage which varies from one comet to another.

One important thing that I used throughout the game was to get acknowledged of the comet by clicking on it so as to assess how severe it is form my community and to adapt the level of protection to it.

I had to deal with a number of trade-offs related to the respect of the horizon, the guarantee of a good level of protection, the cost and the repair of the damage.

If I had played the game only once, I would research more about the comets the next time, pay more attention to the horizon and act quickly to reach the level of the line at the left. In fact, the game has taught me that a good knowledge accompanied with a good preparation is the basic tools to alleviate or get rid of the damage caused by risks.

My proposition concerning the game is to focus on a single level and to work on a real case. To be more explicit, we can study a real risk that had occurred in developing country for example, since they are the most vulnerable, and to let the student suggest what he would have do if he has been a decision maker and compare his decisions in the end with the measures that were taken in reality.
