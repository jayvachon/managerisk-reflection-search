This game enabled me to fully understand the implications of the trade-offs between knowledge, protection and insurance when it came to guarding against the meteoroids. Because of the limitations which I had on firstly, time, and secondly, money, I had to think quickly and make decisions promptly when the meteoroids were coming to my planet. I would have liked to spend more time than I did on researching the meteoroids but then I would have to compromise on insurance and protection (and development of my planet). 

I found that if I kept an adequate level of protection (approximately the same number of bars that was the intensity of the meteoroid) then most of the time there were no damages. Thus I found protection especially important. Since I was always well protected I usually only bought 33% insurance and not more than that. I felt that this strategy was effective. I learnt this by playing the game twice and I also learnt as I progressed from level to level. 

I wanted to be ambitious and keep upgrading and building up the pods but the main constraint was money. I had to wait for the money to replenish, but often by the time it did I had to spend on protection or insurance and had to compromise on development. I learnt to manage time and money more efficiently as I kept playing the game. 

Even though there were constraints however, I felt that the money was being replenished quite fast and after playing the game once or twice it became easy and I didn’t have to be as stressed out as before and did not have to always be on edge. I think the game can be made more realistic by adding more constraints on money. Living in a developing country (India), one of the main constraints, which prevents the individuals, the community and the government in this country from investing more in knowledge, protection, and insurance is the resource constraint. 

I would suggest that the monetary endowment in this game be replenished at a slower rate or maybe the player would have to take part in some economic activity to generate this money. This could be added to make the game more challenging and realistic. Adding to this resource constraint another thought I had was if another planet (or planets) were introduced and possibly lend or borrow money from each other. This would just be to add another dimension to the game even though it is currently already quite dynamic.
