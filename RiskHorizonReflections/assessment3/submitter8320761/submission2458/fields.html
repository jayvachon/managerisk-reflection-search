1.  

The role of risk management in Risk Horizon was to allow development to continually progress in the face of shocks through the adoption of risk management tools (“powers”): knowledge (“research”), protection and insurance. Knowledge and protection mitigated and in some cases prevented shocks, while insurance assisted in coping after a shock occurred. However these tools were required to be adopted strategically.


Choices were required to decide whether and to what extent risk could be managed and prepared for versus taking action after the occurrence of a shock. Further, a continual cost-benefit analysis was necessary to balance: the costs of developing the Alora community; the costs – whether financial or to the development bar – of preparation and prevention measures; and having funds available for post-shock repairs. Lastly, time management was also an integral factor to consider.


Each risk management tool had a financial cost that lessened the amount that could be spent on building or upgrading pods to grow the community. The growth of the community itself was something that required time in order to achieve. Protection had the capacity to reduce the long-term effects of a shock but not without a financial cost, a reduction in the rate of development and time constraints. The accumulation of research, with the right time and cost management, was a powerful and cheap option to plan for, mitigate and in some instances protect from shocks. Finally, the benefit of insurance – despite the outlay having no immediate benefits, high initial costs, and no assured return on investment – by and large reduced the overall expenditure and timeframes of coping with damages after a shock.



2.  

I played Risk Horizon multiple times and the main lesson I learned from the game was the importance of understanding the nature of the situation (ie. the game and it's rules) in a timely manner. Without having read “How to Play” first and launching straight into “Play” I made no effort to prepare myself for what I was about to experience. My first attempts resulted in a fair amount of trial and error, taking many uneducated and unnecessary risks and not recognising and taking advantage of opportunities when they presented. In general I improved with each subsequent game. I say “in general” as I also learned that regardless of how well prepared I was, some shocks were unexpectedly large and devastating even with risk management tools in place.


On reflection, this likely reflects some real world scenarios where failure to prepare for a shock is caused by lack of background understanding coupled with the inability to recognise both risks and opportunities; as well as the reality that shocks can be costly and cannot always be wholly prevented. This game has afforded me a better understanding of this fact. I have also come to better appreciate the complexity of risk management.
