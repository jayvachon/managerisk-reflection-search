1)      In this game the role of risk management was how I would allocate my resources: the time and the money. According to the information I had about every comet and especially about the probabilities of hitting and the impact of crashing, I ought to decide if I would spend my resources on the development, the protection or the insurance. On the first couple of levels, emphasis was given to the development and the achievement of a decent level of protection (3-4 grades). The preparation for the risks did affect the development. Most of the time was consumed for research and protection improvement. This time part was increasing in every higher level.    

2)      I played the game many times. First of all I had to understand its reasoning well and then to adapt my strategy accordingly. What I did different comparing to the first times? On the first times I tried to explore the probabilities of hitting and its impact. On my last effort I had a specific strategy of risk and resources strategy and with some more luck than on previous times, I reached level 5. What I learned was that I should be quick on getting and estimating any information but also on deciding between alternatives. Risk is everywhere in our lives and we should not underestimate it.

3)      My place where I live has been hit harshly by the economic crisis. Therefore, in the game I would add a factor of financial instability. Every pod would need different time and resources to get developed. Consequently, it would contribute different amount of income to the community. Consequently, after a comet hit, each pod would have different priority to be fixed again. This would represent better the fact that in times of economic crisis and instability, some domains are more important than others and most of the limited available resources would be spent to them.
