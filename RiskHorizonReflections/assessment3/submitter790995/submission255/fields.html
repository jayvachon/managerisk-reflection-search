The ability to manage risk was important in achieving success for the game.  At any given time, I could only partake in one of two actions: to build and upgrade the pods OR to protect against potential danger from the comets.  The decision to generate protection meant that I was forgoing the option to develop the pods.  Both options cost time and money and unfortunately, there was a limited supply of both.  If I didn’t build enough pods, I would not reach the required level of development before time ran out.  However, if I spent too much time and money building pods, the unavoidable comets would destroy the island and I would not have enough time and money to repair the damage.  In terms of protecting the island, knowledge didn’t cost any money but it took time to research the comet.  Protection cost both time and money.  Insurance could be purchased instantaneously but cost a lot of money.   

I played the game multiple times and improved with each attempt.  Instead of purchasing insurance right away, I spent more of my efforts on research and protection.  With enough of both, I was able to avoid both incurring damage and purchasing insurance for all rounds except for the final round.  I made the decision to purchase insurance for the final round because the high cost of pod repairs after a comet hit would bankrupt me and prevent the meter from filling up before time ran out.  I learned that it was important to build and upgrade just enough pods to keep the meter at a healthy green level.  Also, since I became quite skilled at creating community connections in the mini game, I didn’t have to spend as much resources on research and protection.  In my experience, it was more advantageous to spend 60% of the time generating protection and 40% on research.  If I could change one aspect of the game, I would increase the cost of the insurance as the number of comets hitting the island increased and as time lapsed in the round.  This option more resembles reality.  For example, medical insurance costs more for an older, unhealthy person than a younger, healthy person.
