The role of risk management in the game was to realize that there is a constant threat which cannot be prevented completely but which can be mitigated to a certain extend. In the game the threat could be managed at three different levels: Knowledge, Protection and Insurance but only a balanced use of the three methods would lead to sustainable development. With the limited time which was given I tried to focus increasingly on the building and upgrading of structures and on the direct Protection by lifting the island up which means that I mostly neglected the aspects of Insurance and Knowledge in the beginning and focused on them increasingly once I had at least 5 structures. In the beginning when one or more prevention measures were applied the development slowed down or even back-warded but it was the key to success on a later stage when the meteorites did leave only limited impact or no impact at all!

 

As I played the game several times I can say that my strategy was completely different the first time(when I failed the completion of the levels) and the second time and third time. The first time I focused mainly on building as many structures as possible and try to lift up the island as much as possible which had negative effects as it was setting the development back and therefore I did not manage to complete the level. The second and third time I used a more balanced approach  by lifting the island, especially in the moment just before a disaster and when I was sure that I still had enough time for 'developing' enough in order to complete the level successfully.
The most important lesson I learned from the game is that prevention, mitigation and recovery of and from a disaster are costly and hinder development and that it is therefore important to use one's resources in the most efficient way in order to achieve the most sustainable development possible.

Furthermore I learned that the income generated by structures is higher if the structures are more developed than if there are many low-developed structures ( Quality more beneficial than Quantity).

                                        

If I would have to develop a similar game I would add the aspect of Man-made disaster. Many disasters are created by mother nature but many are created directly by humans and their actions.
In the countries I have lived the major problems and threats which hindered sustainable development were man-made. Even natural disasters which are related to climate change can be associated to humanity's actions as Climate Change has proven to be partly the consequence of society's impacts on the planet earth.

Man-made disasters could for instance be 'riots in society', 'war related poverty', 'air pollution' and 'water pollution' and prevention and mitigation measures for it could be 'research', 'mainstreaming' and 'efficient politics'.
