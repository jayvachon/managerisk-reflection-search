Risk Horizon-Game

When I started playing the game I felt strongly to understand the basic nature of the risk of Alora Island i.e. the comets hitting the island, the data relating to the frequency of the comet hits, severity of the damages caused and the research done in this regard, thus establishing a balance between preparing for the risk in developing Alora community but also thinking about the different tools available with us. In the beginning itself I realised that I had limited resources at my disposal and it was not enough to rely on insurance after knowing the fact that I  needed to invest on protection also because insurance covers part of the damages caused. On the other hand if I spent too much on building and upgrading pods then my community was exposed to comet hits.  

The game gave the feeling like “sandbox of God” where everything could be controlled except the chance of getting damages to the island. As there were limited resources, there was a tension between spending upfront on risk management (for shocks that could hit the future but could not hit) or to spend on other development priorities. While playing the game, struggling to get past level 4 made my concepts stronger and logical such as prioritizing the things to be done, to view micro and macro aspects of life, what does it mean to have control over the resources and visualisation of the result fills one with all the feeling of fearful, thrilled and anxious kept me vigilant.

As the game started, I was given with 1000 $, 3.00 minutes, an island and a beautiful sky (with no comets of course). My first objective was to build the 4-5 pods as quickly as possible. Gradually, I filled the whole island with pods and developed them all. During the building time of the pods, I put my all effort to provide the protection as much as possible. As soon as comets came in the view I researched them for severity and chances of hitting. If the comet hit then I tried to cover the damages done already by clicking the green fog on the pods. I used to cover the risk by protecting the pods first and to get them insured at the third level when the chances of hitting were more. As the pods were growing in some very beautiful structures, I was more inclining towards protection and insurance both.

The lessen given by the RiskHorizon game was that development is first, development, research and protection are simultaneously second and development, research and insurance are simultaneously third. All the risks always cannot be covered or controlled but it can be minimized by the wise and experienced vision.

I played the game many a times. To begin with I depended more on the facility of insurance for protecting the community/island but with this approach I was left with little resources for investing on development, protection and research. Then after I started to build and upgrade as many pods as I could and provided proper protection to them within the given the time limit and before any chance of the comets hitting and damaging. Simultaneously, I kept on watching on the movement of the comet, their severity and chances of hitting the island so as to take a decision to select a proper size of the insurance cover at an appropriate time.

The better version of the game may provide parallel research option with the development and protection of the pods.
