In my opinion, the game Risk Horizon was a pretty impressive way of imparting awareness of what risk management is and how critical it is, in order for development to take place. Risk management in the game was basically about alertness of incoming obstacles (through research), providing security (by investing in insurance) when disasters hit and preventing damage (by acquiring protection) to avoid the risk. I played the game more than once and overtime I was able to work out and prioritise what needed to be done at different stages of the level.

 In every game I played, I made sure I purchased insurance. There really was no question with regard to investing in insurance. It was a cost-effective approach that would benefit the development process throughout the level and towards managing the risk made by the comet strike. In the long run I saved more money by investing in insurance than I would have had I not. I would have spent more money paying all the damage costs, on my own. When deciding how much protection to acquire, my decision was heavily dependent on the data, through research, that indicated the severity and probability of the comet hitting. I also depended on research greatly because acquiring protection halted development from taking place so I needed to establish whether or not it was worth the shot and also whether it was very necessary.

Some of the main things I learnt from the game are:

 Ø  Research is a very important aspect of risk management and plays a very critical role in helping determine what measure of risk management should be taken at different stages of risk management and how much of it should be taken. Of course this is greatly dependent on its severity and the probability of the crisis hitting revealed through data collected by research. This not only allows recovery after the disaster or prevention of risk taking place but it also saves money and time.

Ø  A preventative measure such as acquiring protection is a good approach to take because it avoids damages and the devastating effect of crisis. However it halts (even prevents) development and as stated throughout the course, certain risks must be taken to allow change and development. Protection therefore should only be acquired when absolutely necessary and when the severity and chances of the disaster hitting is very high.

Ø  It is always wise to invest in insurance to provide a cushion of security especially in terms of funding towards recovery after adversity, if preventative measures fail. Investment in insurance is a long-term commitment thus decision of how much money should be spent on it should be based also on information acquired through research.

If there was one way I could improve the game that would be through changing the bar representing protection and replacing it with a notification icon that states how much protection has been acquired, in figures (percentage). That would be a more efficient way of indicating to the gamer how much has been acquired. Otherwise, playing Risk Horizon helped me visualize everything I have been learning throughout the course and helped me analyse certain aspects of it. It helped me understand how important it is to prioritise the different risk management approaches and consider the impact they would have on development process. When I finally got myself engrossed in the game, I also understood the importance of time management factor in the entire processes of both development and risk management.
