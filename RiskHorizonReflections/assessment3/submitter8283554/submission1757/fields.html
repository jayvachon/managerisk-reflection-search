Not being a game person made me play the game several times but with each game I improved my strategies. I began to understand the principle of community connection through community development (pod building), researching, protecting the community by providing some elevation and buying insurance to enable quick repair of damage to community. This is similar with real life cases, the first time a particular type of shock strikes the impacts can be devastating but with repeat events and improved preparation, impacts are better managed. Risk management is a ‘never a dull moment’ management technique. To overcome damages, I (Luminator) had to be active researching, protecting or enhancing insurance through building/repairing pods.

Playing the game I appreciated the important of preparing for foreseeable shocks to limit damages. To overcome shocks a good balance of knowledge, protection and insurance (including community connections) is necessary.

Knowledge acquired through research helped me (Luminator) make appropriate decision and implement necessary remediation plan - community protection and insurance (including community interaction). In contrast, acquiring all the knowledge and not implementing necessary strategic actions amounts to nothing.

Protecting communities that have taken years of effort and dedication to develop is unarguably essential. Since all shocks cannot be prevented, I discovered that protection goes a long way to limit impacts and an extra protection goes a longer way. Additional protection deals with the deep uncertainties associated with disasters. With adequate protection, damages are less, repairs easier and opportunities for prosperity possible.

Insurance takes care of property restoration and removes pressure on property owners (in real life situation). In the game, the importance of insurance for development was played out. Without insurance, reconstruction of pods become difficult - takes a longer time.

Economic empowerment and financial resource is importance for effective risk management. As played out in the game, without sufficient financial resources pods cannot be built, insurance cannot be purchased and repairs cannot be made. Since community cohesion an important form of insurance can only be achieved through developing communities (building pods), the need for financial resource is inevitable. This is to say that the components of adaptive capacity (risk management) require some measure of economic capability.

Finally, the game instructions are not clear and influence of added protection on the overall game performance is not sufficient. Practically, protection goes a long way but this is not same for Alora community. In making a change to the game, I will improve the influence protection has on a Luminator developing Alora community and moving to the next level of the game.
