I thoroughly enjoyed playing the Risk Horizon game as it helped refined my risk management skill-set.  Two words immediately come to my mind when I consider the gaming experience:  diversification and multi-tasking.  When I began playing the game, I recalled  from the World Development Report that it would take a diverse set of risk management strategies in order to succeed, so I tried all of my options in the game. Knowledge, protection, and insurance were all mandatory techniques for me.  I remembered that all opportunity must be considered as well as all risk, so I immediately started building communities in order to advance my income level.  Once new communities were built, and my income level was sufficient, I upgraded them immediately, as I knew that upgrades would increase my rate of return on development.  Despite the fact that I started with the goal of rapid development as my upmost priority, I was well aware of the potential loss I could incur from a comet impact.  I tempered the rate at which I built new communities in the first two levels and spread out my income spending by purchasing the highest level of insurance and raising my communities to prevent damage from comet impact.  When my income was depleted from the spending, I thought that I would be best time to focus on researching incoming comets as that required no additional income.   I primarily alternated between researching comets and raising the communities. Where the comets had a low probability of impact when they were halfway across the sky, I ceased research activity.  If there was more than one comet I focused on the first comet until I reached at least 3 indication bars and then focused on the additional comets.  When a comet was more than 3/4ths of the way across the sky and destined for impact, I made sure I had at least 4 bars of research towards it. Despite attention given to protection and knowledge, I did not keep rapid development out of mind for I built new communities and made upgrades as my income level surpassed a certain threshold.   My tactics proved to be consistently successful which the key indicator that my risk management mechanism was quite effective.

I played the game several times, and adapted my strategies each time I played.  The first time I lost the game was playing on Level 3 when I was overly concerned with protection and knowledge that I did not meet the required level to move to Level 4.  I altered my strategy so that I would develop faster—building more communities and upgrading them more.  I used my income level as an indication meter—if I had more than $600, I felt as if I was underutilizing my development resources.

Overall, I was impressed by the gameplay and game design however if I were re-designing the game, I would place more of an emphasis on infrastructural development and the acquisition of knowledge.  I found the communication period between communities after a comet impact to be quite unrealistic—not being ability to distinctly measure its utility in the game.  Also, I felt as if comet research should realistically cost an income amount in the gameplay.  An additional suggestion would be encouraging building infrastructural connections between communities to strengthen resources of protection and institutions that would increase and sustain knowledge.
