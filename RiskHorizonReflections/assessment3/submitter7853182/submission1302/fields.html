Risk Horizon teaches us what risk management is like in the real world; albeit with an element of fantasy in it to make it more lively and appealing.

The role of risk management that we had to face was essentially a trade-off. The three aspects of Knowledge, Protection and Insurance had to be balanced in such a way that Alura had enough resources for development too. The fundamental role was that of balancing everything, as further explained:

Knowledge: Knowing your risk is vital so as to be prepared for the impending risk and face it without incurring any sort of loss. In Risk Horizon though, I learnt that if you spend too much time solely focusing on gaining knowledge (Research) by watching the meteors, you will lose out on precious time which can be spent on development or creation of new opportunities. Knowing your risk is important, and so is bracing yourself to face the same; but being too engrossed in it will be of little use. At the end of the day, it is essential that you balance your preparation with healthy and holistic development.
Another aspect is knowing if the risk is sever enough to protect against. Sometimes an impending risk may not really affect you much; in which case researching or preparing against it will prove a mere waste of valuable time as well as resources.

 

Protection: After knowledge, comes protection. Here we again face a trade-off, whether to develop or to protect. Either way, our resources are being used and so is our time. But should we really devote so much into protecting that we hinder our own progress? Striking the right balance is what Risk Horizon, and indeed the concept of Risk Management teaches us. In the game, if the comet’s severity wasn’t too high, extreme levels of expensive protection wasn’t needed. If the severity of the risk is too high or if the risk is such that no protection can be sought, there exists two possible solutions – building connections and insurance. The latter will be further explained, while the former concept is essentially a way of ensuring that no risk leaves us shorthanded. It is merely another way of preparing for any risk. By building connections, you can ensure that no matter how severe the risk hits you, there’s always a way out. Development is easier when such connections are in existence.

 

Insurance: There are many risks against which you simply cannot protect yourself with no matter what. These risks will leave you at a loss and cannot be prevented or even foreseen. For such times, there is insurance. Insurance may be defined as the act of valuing a risk and shifting the element of risk to an insurer who will be taking the risk at a premium paid by the customer. Insurance helps people to better deal with risks. The nature of risks means that the chance of that risk occurring is fluctuating, which benefits the insurers and the insured as well. But depending on insurance is also not a viable option, there always needs to be a balance between this and the aforementioned elements.

 

As indicated above, the main things I learnt from the game are why striking a balance is important in order for risk to be managed and development to continue, and how one can strike such a balance. The first time was a little intimidating as balancing the various aspects of risk management seemed a bit daunting, but as I gave it a few more tries, I understood the concept better eventually just missing out on level 4.
